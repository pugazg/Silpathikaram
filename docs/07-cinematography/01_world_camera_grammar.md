# World Camera Grammar

## Pukār

### Visual identity

A living maritime city before loss.

### Camera rules

- Camera observes systems before individuals.
- Wide frames establish labour networks: harbour, merchants, performers and households.
- Movement follows exchange routes rather than only emotional characters.
- Crowds should contain profession and purpose.

### Light

Morning river light, festival illumination and domestic lamp systems should reveal different social spaces.

### Constraint

Do not photograph Pukār as a fantasy palace disconnected from trade and labour.

## Road

### Visual identity

Transformation through distance.

### Camera rules

- Greater emphasis on physical effort.
- Horizon and terrain become active forces.
- Repeated walking compositions show accumulated change.
- Close observation of feet, water, food and rest points.

### Constraint

The landscape must not become empty adventure scenery.

## Madurai

### Visual identity

A city of authority, procedure and failed justice.

### Camera rules

- Architecture creates hierarchy.
- Court framing should allow evidence, witnesses and power relationships to remain visible.
- The anklet receives visual priority during proof.

### Constraint

The execution and fire must preserve wrongful judgement as the political centre.

## Vañci

### Visual identity

Memory becoming institution.

### Camera rules

- Move from individual witness to collective ritual.
- Communities who preserve memory remain visible.
- Royal ceremony is a continuation of testimony, not its replacement.

### Constraint

Do not allow monument construction to erase earlier recognition.
