# Visual Motif and Sound Continuity

## Anklet

The anklet evolves through states:

1. Household wealth.
2. Surviving capital.
3. Accused object.
4. Evidence.
5. Memory object.

Camera rule: never introduce the anklet as only a magical symbol. Its material history creates meaning.

## Water

Water marks transition:

- Kāviri and departure.
- Travel crossings.
- Ritual and recognition.

Sound rule: water should change with location and emotional state.

## Fire

Fire is not spectacle alone.

Visual progression:

- domestic flame;
- civic ritual flame;
- selective destruction;
- remembered sacred flame.

## Footsteps

Footsteps track transformation:

- protected household movement;
- road exhaustion;
- public approach to justice;
- remembered pilgrimage.

## Music

Music follows social function:

- yāḻ as artistic language;
- performance as public institution;
- song as misunderstood communication;
- memory carried through later testimony.

## Silence

Silence becomes important after irreversible events. It should not simply indicate absence of dialogue; it marks changed relationships between person, city and memory.

## Soundscape principle

Every major location needs its own acoustic identity:

- Pukār: harbour, markets, performance, households.
- Road: wind, terrain, animals, footsteps and limited human density.
- Madurai: markets, gates, court procedure and civic alarm.
- Vañci: ritual, collective memory and institutional sound.
