# Road Scene Architecture — Part 2

## Scope

Scenes `SCN-ROAD-007`–`SCN-ROAD-012` carry the travellers through seasonal pālai, competing sacred routes, illusion, heat and the hunter community’s ritual world.

## SCN-ROAD-007 — Pālai is made by season

- **Canto:** 11. Seeing the Forest
- **Location / time:** Summer-transformed mullai and kuṟiñci terrain; day
- **Source:** HWT-0069, HWT-0070
- **Characters:** Kaṇṇaki, Kōvalaṉ, Kauṇṭi
- **Form / lock:** SOURCE_COMPOSITE · LOCKED_SOURCE
- **Source action:** Summer transforms familiar landscapes into pālai; dry trees, bamboo, thirsty deer and hunter territory lead toward cultivated hills.
- **Dramatic function:** Make ecology causal and unstable rather than assign one permanent desert geography.
- **Turn:** Heat becomes the dominant force governing pace and route.
- **Performance focus:** Water calculation, shade seeking, altered breath and reduced conversation.
- **Production requirements:** Seasonal landscape continuity, heat effects, animal signs and safe performance under harsh light.
- **Transition out:** The difficult terrain makes the miraculous cave route sound briefly attractive.
- **Interpretive limit:** Do not visualise pālai as an endless sand desert.

## SCN-ROAD-008 — The cave route promises knowledge

- **Canto:** 11. Seeing the Forest
- **Location / time:** Route discussion near the mountain path; day
- **Source:** HWT-0071
- **Characters / institutions:** Travellers, travelling Brahmin, guarded cave and three sacred ponds
- **Form / lock:** MIXED_PERFORMANCE · RESEARCH_HOLD
- **Source action:** A cave route is associated with guarded thresholds, mantras and ponds promising knowledge, memory or accomplishment.
- **Dramatic function:** Present spiritual possibility as a real competing route choice.
- **Turn:** The journey could divert from practical survival into sacred quest.
- **Performance focus:** Wonder is balanced by Kaṇṇaki’s exhaustion and Kauṇṭi’s scrutiny.
- **Production requirements:** Cave geography, threshold ritual, water sites and historically cautious sacred design.
- **Transition out:** Kauṇṭi rejects the detour and defines the journey’s ethical priority.
- **Interpretive limit:** Do not confirm the promised powers as objective cinematic fact.

## SCN-ROAD-009 — Kauṇṭi chooses the direct road

- **Canto:** 11. Seeing the Forest
- **Location / time:** Branching road; continuous
- **Source:** HWT-0072, HWT-0073
- **Characters:** Kauṇṭi, Kaṇṇaki, Kōvalaṉ
- **Form / lock:** SOURCE_DIRECT · LOCKED_SOURCE
- **Source action:** Kauṇṭi rejects the cave quest and chooses direct travel grounded in truthful conduct and protection of life.
- **Dramatic function:** Establish ethical route-making as action.
- **Turn:** Kauṇṭi’s judgement governs the next stage despite Kōvalaṉ’s destination-driven urgency.
- **Performance focus:** She listens fully, decides briefly and resumes walking.
- **Production requirements:** Clear route fork, minimal dialogue staging and visible bodily condition.
- **Transition out:** The direct route reaches a pond where appearance becomes unreliable.
- **Interpretive limit:** Do not turn her refusal into ridicule of another tradition.

## SCN-ROAD-010 — The forest speaks in a familiar face

- **Canto:** 11. Seeing the Forest
- **Location / time:** Forest pond; later day
- **Source:** HWT-0074
- **Characters:** Kōvalaṉ, forest deity in Vasantamālai’s form, Kaṇṇaki, Kauṇṭi
- **Form / lock:** MIXED_PERFORMANCE · DEVELOPMENT
- **Source action:** A forest deity assumes Vasantamālai’s appearance, invokes Mādhavi and is exposed through Kōvalaṉ’s protective mantra.
- **Dramatic function:** Externalise how unresolved Pukār memory follows the travellers without making memory itself the villain.
- **Turn:** Kōvalaṉ recognises deception but is forced to confront the past he has not resolved.
- **Performance focus:** Recognition, doubt and controlled recitation rather than action-horror staging.
- **Production requirements:** Performance-based doubling, restrained transformation effects, pond reflections and sound discontinuity.
- **Transition out:** Heat and fatigue return immediately after the illusion breaks.
- **Interpretive limit:** Do not merge the forest deity with Mādhavi or treat Mādhavi as supernatural threat.

## SCN-ROAD-011 — Heat drives them to Aiyai’s enclosure

- **Canto:** 11. Seeing the Forest; 12. Hunter Song
- **Location / time:** Hunter territory and Aiyai-Korravai shrine; evening
- **Source:** HWT-0075, HWT-0076
- **Characters:** Kaṇṇaki, Kōvalaṉ, Kauṇṭi, hunter community
- **Form / lock:** SOURCE_COMPOSITE · LOCKED_SOURCE
- **Source action:** Day heat makes travel difficult; Kaṇṇaki’s feet redden and the group rests in the shrine enclosure.
- **Dramatic function:** Bring her bodily cost to its clearest pre-Madurai point.
- **Turn:** The travellers must accept protection from a community outside their own social world.
- **Performance focus:** Foot washing, inability to conceal pain, Kauṇṭi’s practical intervention and Kōvalaṉ’s recognition of the cost.
- **Production requirements:** Shrine enclosure, travel-body makeup, water and rest actions, hunter settlement geography.
- **Transition out:** Communal distress erupts through Sālini’s possession.
- **Interpretive limit:** Do not reduce the hunter settlement to danger or exotic atmosphere.

## SCN-ROAD-012 — Sālini names a future no one understands

- **Canto:** 12. Hunter Song
- **Location / time:** Aiyai-Korravai ritual enclosure; night
- **Source:** HWT-0077, HWT-0078, HWT-0079, HWT-0080
- **Characters / institutions:** Sālini, ritual maiden, hunter community, Kaṇṇaki, Kōvalaṉ, Kauṇṭi
- **Form / lock:** MIXED_PERFORMANCE · RESEARCH_HOLD
- **Source action:** Possession declares an unpaid ritual debt; the community transforms a maiden through costume, offerings, sound and procession; Sālini identifies Kaṇṇaki as an exceptional southern woman.
- **Dramatic function:** Give Kaṇṇaki anticipatory recognition inside a specific communal ritual before the legal proof in Madurai.
- **Turn:** Others perceive significance in Kaṇṇaki that she does not yet claim for herself.
- **Performance focus:** Communal coordination and Kaṇṇaki’s discomfort or incomprehension, not a triumphant revelation.
- **Production requirements:** Ritual research, drums, horn, flute, bell, offerings, animal symbols and movement safety.
- **Transition out:** Ritual sound fades into moonlit travel under Pāṇḍya order.
- **Interpretive limit:** Recognition must not turn Kaṇṇaki into an already conscious goddess or substitute for later evidence.
