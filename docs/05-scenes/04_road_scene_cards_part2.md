# Road Scene Architecture — Part 2

## Scope

Scenes `SCN-ROAD-010`–`SCN-ROAD-018` complete the journey into Madurai through hunter ritual, sacred encounter, outer settlements and the Vaigai threshold.

## SCN-ROAD-010 — Hunter world and communal ritual

- **Source:** HWT-0070–HWT-0078
- **Function:** Reveal a functioning forest community rather than an exotic obstacle.
- **Turn:** Travel encounters another social order with its own sacred obligations.

## SCN-ROAD-011 — Sālini recognises before the city does

- **Source:** HWT-0079
- **Function:** Introduce early recognition of Kaṇṇaki’s extraordinary status.
- **Limit:** Recognition must not erase human uncertainty.

## SCN-ROAD-012 — Leaving the forest community

- **Source:** HWT-0080–HWT-0082
- **Function:** Carry memory and warning toward Madurai.

## SCN-ROAD-013 — Night movement toward the capital

- **Source:** HWT-0083
- **Function:** Show exhaustion and continued movement.

## SCN-ROAD-014 — Outer settlement refuge

- **Source:** HWT-0084–HWT-0086
- **Function:** Prepare the final separation between traveller and city institutions.

## SCN-ROAD-015 — Messenger network crosses back

- **Source:** HWT-0087
- **Function:** Connect Madurai arrival with consequences left in Pukār.

## SCN-ROAD-016 — Vaigai threshold

- **Source:** HWT-0088
- **Function:** Present Madurai as an organised political landscape.

## SCN-ROAD-017 — The capital appears

- **Source:** HWT-0089
- **Function:** End travel and begin city architecture.

## SCN-ROAD-018 — Entry into Madurai sequence

- **Form:** CONTROLLED_BRIDGE · LOCKED_ADAPTATION
- **Function:** Transition from landscape drama into institutional drama.
