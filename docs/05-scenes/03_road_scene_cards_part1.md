# Road Scene Architecture — Part 1

## Scope

Scenes `SCN-ROAD-001`–`SCN-ROAD-006` transform the protected merchant couple into exposed travellers. Cultivated terrain, agrarian labour, ascetic teaching and route knowledge replace the social protections of Pukār.

## SCN-ROAD-001 — The ground refuses confidence

- **Canto:** 10. Seeing the Country
- **Location / time:** Orchards and cultivated paths beyond Pukār; morning
- **Source:** HWT-0061
- **Characters:** Kaṇṇaki, Kōvalaṉ, Kauṇṭi
- **Form / lock:** SOURCE_DIRECT · LOCKED_SOURCE
- **Source action:** Pits, fallen fruit, fish, bees, crabs and other hazards make even cultivated land difficult to cross.
- **Dramatic function:** Announce that social status has no authority over terrain.
- **Turn:** Kauṇṭi’s practical knowledge becomes necessary.
- **Performance focus:** Kaṇṇaki tests each step; Kōvalaṉ’s forward confidence is interrupted; Kauṇṭi redirects them without ceremony.
- **Production requirements:** Working orchard paths, concealed hazards, travel footwear continuity and close sound of insects and water.
- **Transition out:** Careful foot placement opens into the wider Kāviri labour world.
- **Interpretive limit:** Do not exaggerate the hazards into adventure spectacle.

## SCN-ROAD-002 — Kāviri works around them

- **Canto:** 10. Seeing the Country
- **Location / time:** Kāviri fields and water channels; day
- **Source:** HWT-0062
- **Characters / institutions:** Travellers, farmers, cattle workers, harvest labourers
- **Form / lock:** MIXED_PERFORMANCE · LOCKED_ADAPTATION
- **Source action:** Floodwater, birds, cattle, field work, songs, drums and ritual smoke create an agrarian soundscape.
- **Dramatic function:** Show that the road crosses a productive society rather than empty scenery.
- **Turn:** The travellers become minor figures inside labour rhythms they do not control.
- **Performance focus:** Movement must negotiate animals, water, tools and workers rather than pass through a decorative landscape.
- **Production requirements:** Irrigation, crops, cattle, harvest actions, field songs and layered rural sound.
- **Transition out:** Repeated labour rhythms become repeated halts along the route.
- **Interpretive limit:** Avoid treating agrarian people as silent background extras.

## SCN-ROAD-003 — Distance is counted by halts

- **Canto:** 10. Seeing the Country
- **Location / time:** Settlements and roadside resting places; several days
- **Source:** HWT-0063
- **Characters:** Kaṇṇaki, Kōvalaṉ, Kauṇṭi, settlement residents
- **Form / lock:** SOURCE_COMPOSITE · LOCKED_ADAPTATION
- **Source action:** The group travels slowly through inhabited country and stops repeatedly.
- **Dramatic function:** Make duration and accumulated bodily cost visible.
- **Turn:** The journey ceases to be Kōvalaṉ’s simple plan and becomes a shared endurance test.
- **Performance focus:** Repacking, washing feet, sleeping lightly and restarting before full recovery.
- **Production requirements:** Costume wear progression, foot continuity, changing shelters and day-count visual logic.
- **Transition out:** A halt at a river island becomes an encounter with ascetic teaching.
- **Interpretive limit:** The exact number and placement of halts are adaptation structure, not asserted textual chronology.

## SCN-ROAD-004 — Teaching on the river island

- **Canto:** 10. Seeing the Country
- **Location / time:** River-side sacred place; day
- **Source:** HWT-0064
- **Characters / institutions:** Kauṇṭi, Kaṇṇaki, Kōvalaṉ, Sāraṇar teachers
- **Form / lock:** MIXED_PERFORMANCE · DEVELOPMENT
- **Source action:** Ascetic teachers speak about karma and liberation; Kauṇṭi states her disciplined commitment.
- **Dramatic function:** Establish a philosophical frame without using fate to excuse later human decisions.
- **Turn:** Kauṇṭi is revealed as a participant in a specific ethical tradition, not a generic guide.
- **Performance focus:** Listening, restraint and doctrinal confidence rather than theatrical prophecy.
- **Production requirements:** River-island geography, ascetic practices, speech rhythm and historically reviewed implements.
- **Transition out:** Teaching ends; the group must still cross and continue physically.
- **Interpretive limit:** Do not make the teachers predict Kōvalaṉ’s death or Madurai’s destruction.

## SCN-ROAD-005 — The south bank removes shelter

- **Canto:** 10. Seeing the Country; 11. Seeing the Forest
- **Location / time:** South bank and flower grove; dawn after rest
- **Source:** HWT-0065, HWT-0066
- **Characters:** Kaṇṇaki, Kōvalaṉ, Kauṇṭi, unfamiliar passers-by
- **Form / lock:** SOURCE_COMPOSITE · LOCKED_SOURCE
- **Source action:** They cross to the south bank, enter a grove and resume the southward journey at dawn.
- **Dramatic function:** Move from familiar Kāviri country into increasing social vulnerability.
- **Turn:** The group is now exposed to strangers without household or civic protection.
- **Performance focus:** Kauṇṭi manages proximity and rest while Kōvalaṉ tries to maintain purpose.
- **Production requirements:** River crossing, grove, wet-to-dry costume continuity and dawn travel.
- **Transition out:** A travelling Brahmin offers political geography and route knowledge.
- **Interpretive limit:** Do not invent an assault or pursuit merely to illustrate vulnerability.

## SCN-ROAD-006 — The road is argued before it is taken

- **Canto:** 11. Seeing the Forest
- **Location / time:** Roadside meeting in Pāṇḍya territory; day
- **Source:** HWT-0067, HWT-0068
- **Characters / institutions:** Travellers, travelling Brahmin, remembered Pāṇḍya order
- **Form / lock:** SOURCE_COMPOSITE · DEVELOPMENT
- **Source action:** Praise links Pāṇḍya rule with rain and abundance; the Brahmin describes sacred sites and routes to Madurai.
- **Dramatic function:** Present geography as political, ecological and contested knowledge.
- **Turn:** The travellers must choose among routes rather than simply follow a map.
- **Performance focus:** Kōvalaṉ listens for speed and destination; Kauṇṭi listens for risk and ethical cost; Kaṇṇaki’s condition remains the practical limit.
- **Production requirements:** Route diagram logic, oral geographic performance and differentiated landscape cues.
- **Transition out:** The proposed routes open into the seasonally transformed pālai landscape.
- **Interpretive limit:** Royal praise is a source voice within the epic, not neutral proof of historical conditions.
