# Madurai Scene Architecture — Part 3A

## Scope

Scenes `SCN-MDU-021`–`SCN-MDU-025` move from remembered examples of fidelity to Kaṇṇaki’s destruction vow, ritualised accusation, the fire deity’s appearance and selective ignition.

## SCN-MDU-021 — Kaṇṇaki summons remembered women
- **Canto:** 21. Vengeance Garland
- **Location / time:** Outside the royal court and within civic hearing; day
- **Source:** HWT-0131
- **Characters / institutions:** Kaṇṇaki, remembered faithful women, citizens
- **Form / lock:** MIXED_PERFORMANCE · DEVELOPMENT
- **Source action:** Kaṇṇaki invokes women remembered for fidelity demonstrated through ordeal, rescue, sacrifice or transformation.
- **Dramatic function:** Place her claim inside a public ethical memory rather than isolated personal rage.
- **Turn:** Her identity as pattini becomes an argument tested against inherited examples.
- **Performance focus:** Each remembered case changes cadence and bodily control without requiring literal flashback.
- **Production requirements:** Spoken-memory design, recurring object or movement motifs and civic listening space.
- **Transition out:** She declares what she will do if her own claim is true.
- **Interpretive limit:** Do not flatten the remembered women into one generic ideal or present invented biographies as source fact.

## SCN-MDU-022 — The vow includes ruler and city
- **Canto:** 21. Vengeance Garland
- **Location / time:** Madurai public space; continuous
- **Source:** HWT-0132
- **Characters / institutions:** Kaṇṇaki, failed ruler, Madurai as addressed civic body
- **Form / lock:** SOURCE_DIRECT · LOCKED_SOURCE
- **Source action:** Kaṇṇaki declares that if she is truly a pattini she will spare neither the ruler nor Madurai.
- **Dramatic function:** Expand judgement from one dead king to the political community sustained by his order.
- **Turn:** Courtroom proof becomes a city-level moral sentence.
- **Performance focus:** The vow is deliberate and frightening because it follows proof, not because it begins as uncontrolled fury.
- **Production requirements:** Civic geography, gathering witnesses and sound that lets the city hear her.
- **Transition out:** She circles Madurai and transforms accusation into embodied ritual action.
- **Interpretive limit:** Do not frame the vow as uncomplicated heroic revenge.

## SCN-MDU-023 — Three circuits and the embodied accusation
- **Canto:** 21. Vengeance Garland
- **Location / time:** Route around Madurai; day into altered light
- **Source:** HWT-0133
- **Characters / institutions:** Kaṇṇaki, citizens, women, men, gods and ascetics as addressed groups
- **Form / lock:** MIXED_PERFORMANCE · RESEARCH_HOLD
- **Source action:** Kaṇṇaki addresses the city’s communities, circles Madurai three times and casts away her left breast as the embodied act of accusation.
- **Dramatic function:** Make judgement spatial, public and bodily before fire begins.
- **Turn:** Her wounded body becomes the medium through which the city is sentenced.
- **Performance focus:** Repetition, exhaustion and widening public witness; no triumphant superhero posture.
- **Production requirements:** Three-circuit geography, body-effect research, crowd continuity and ritual-movement consultation.
- **Transition out:** The fire deity appears in response to the completed accusation.
- **Interpretive limit:** The body act requires source-critical and production-sensitive treatment; avoid eroticisation or gore spectacle.

## SCN-MDU-024 — Fire appears under prior command
- **Canto:** 21. Vengeance Garland
- **Location / time:** Madurai; immediately after the circuits
- **Source:** HWT-0134, HWT-0135
- **Characters / institutions:** Kaṇṇaki, fire deity in Brahmin form, protected social categories
- **Form / lock:** MIXED_PERFORMANCE · RESEARCH_HOLD
- **Source action:** The fire deity appears and states that a prior command authorised the burning when Madurai wronged the great pattini; Kaṇṇaki names ritual specialists, righteous people, cattle, faithful wives, elders and children as protected.
- **Dramatic function:** Preserve the epic’s sacred dimension while fixing explicit moral limits on destruction.
- **Turn:** Catastrophe receives categories of exemption before ignition spreads.
- **Performance focus:** Kaṇṇaki issues exclusions with precision; the deity listens and acts rather than replacing her judgement.
- **Production requirements:** Fire-deity visual strategy, protected-group staging and non-generic Brahmin-form research.
- **Transition out:** Fire moves toward those aligned with wrongdoing.
- **Interpretive limit:** Do not assimilate the deity automatically to later theology or erase the text’s stated protected groups.

## SCN-MDU-025 — Fire chooses rather than merely spreads
- **Canto:** 21. Vengeance Garland
- **Location / time:** Multiple Madurai zones; continuous
- **Source:** HWT-0136
- **Characters / institutions:** Fire, wrongdoers, protected people and city spaces
- **Form / lock:** SOURCE_DIRECT · LOCKED_SOURCE
- **Source action:** Fire is directed toward those associated with wrongdoing rather than represented as indiscriminate natural disaster.
- **Dramatic function:** Establish the governing rule for every later destruction image.
- **Turn:** The city begins to burn under a selective moral logic that remains catastrophic but bounded.
- **Performance focus:** Human attempts to interpret where fire moves and does not move.
- **Production requirements:** Zone-based fire plan, evacuation choreography, protected-space continuity and VFX/practical-fire integration.
- **Transition out:** Protective and commanding institutions withdraw as the city’s ordinary systems fail.
- **Interpretive limit:** Selectivity must not make the catastrophe painless, clean or morally simple.
