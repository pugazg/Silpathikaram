# Madurai Scene Architecture — Part 2B

## Scope

Scenes `SCN-MDU-016`–`SCN-MDU-020` bring Kaṇṇaki through the palace gate, establish her identity and material claim, test the anklet and collapse Pāṇḍya authority.

## SCN-MDU-016 — The gate receives a claimant, not a petitioner
- **Canto:** 20. Court Case
- **Location / time:** Palace gate and court approach; day
- **Source:** HWT-0124, HWT-0125
- **Characters / institutions:** Kaṇṇaki, gatekeeper, queen’s retinue, palace guards
- **Form / lock:** SOURCE_COMPOSITE · LOCKED_SOURCE
- **Source action:** The queen enters with attendants and ceremonial objects while Kaṇṇaki names the king’s failure and demands admission holding the surviving anklet.
- **Dramatic function:** Collide palace order with an evidence-bearing outsider who refuses deferential framing.
- **Turn:** The gatekeeper must transmit a challenge that cannot be contained as ordinary supplication.
- **Performance focus:** Kaṇṇaki projects only what is needed; the gatekeeper shifts from control to alarmed transmission.
- **Production requirements:** Gate hierarchy, retinue objects, guard movement and sound contrast between ceremony and demand.
- **Transition out:** The king orders or permits her admission.
- **Interpretive limit:** Do not invent prolonged physical combat with guards or effortless supernatural access.

## SCN-MDU-017 — Kaṇṇaki establishes standing
- **Canto:** 20. Court Case
- **Location / time:** Royal court; continuous
- **Source:** HWT-0126
- **Characters / institutions:** Kaṇṇaki, Pāṇḍya king and queen, court officials
- **Form / lock:** SOURCE_DIRECT · LOCKED_SOURCE
- **Source action:** Kaṇṇaki states her name, Pukār origin, merchant lineage, marriage and the purpose of Kōvalaṉ’s entry into Madurai.
- **Dramatic function:** Make identity and economic context part of the legal claim.
- **Turn:** The court must hear Kōvalaṉ as a merchant husband carrying family property, not an anonymous thief.
- **Performance focus:** Controlled sequence, exact facts and refusal to be interrupted by rank.
- **Production requirements:** Court acoustics, sightlines, anklet visibility and restrained official reaction.
- **Transition out:** She states the testable difference between the two anklets.
- **Interpretive limit:** Do not expand the speech into invented biography or modern legal terminology.

## SCN-MDU-018 — Gems against pearls
- **Canto:** 20. Court Case
- **Location / time:** Royal court; continuous
- **Source:** HWT-0127
- **Characters / institutions:** Kaṇṇaki, king, queen, court and anklet
- **Form / lock:** SOURCE_DIRECT · LOCKED_SOURCE
- **Source action:** Kaṇṇaki argues that her anklet contains gems while the queen’s contains pearls.
- **Dramatic function:** Reduce the dispute to a material difference the court could and should have tested earlier.
- **Turn:** Sovereign certainty becomes vulnerable to inspection.
- **Performance focus:** She handles the object as known property; court attention shifts from her social position to its construction.
- **Production requirements:** Matched but internally distinct anklet props, macro photography and material continuity.
- **Transition out:** The anklet is brought forward and broken.
- **Interpretive limit:** The distinction must not be obscured by decorative prop design.

## SCN-MDU-019 — The object completes the argument
- **Canto:** 20. Court Case
- **Location / time:** Royal court; continuous
- **Source:** HWT-0128
- **Characters / institutions:** Kaṇṇaki, king, queen, court witnesses
- **Form / lock:** SOURCE_DIRECT · LOCKED_SOURCE
- **Source action:** Kaṇṇaki breaks the anklet and a gem strikes the king, supplying immediate physical proof.
- **Dramatic function:** Let evidence—not volume, prophecy or confession—defeat the wrongful classification.
- **Turn:** The court’s prior decision becomes publicly indefensible.
- **Performance focus:** Prepared force, stillness after impact and witnesses processing the material fact.
- **Production requirements:** Breakable hero prop, safe gem trajectory, impact timing and sound motif resolution.
- **Transition out:** The king speaks his self-indictment.
- **Interpretive limit:** Do not turn the proof into magical transformation or an action spectacle.

## SCN-MDU-020 — The sceptre indicts itself
- **Canto:** 20. Court Case
- **Location / time:** Royal court; immediate aftermath
- **Source:** HWT-0129, HWT-0130
- **Characters / institutions:** Pāṇḍya king, queen, Kaṇṇaki, court
- **Form / lock:** SOURCE_COMPOSITE · LOCKED_SOURCE
- **Source action:** The king identifies himself as the thief because sovereign failure caused the killing; the queen bows at Kaṇṇaki’s feet and falls, declaring that no consolation exists for the bereaved wife.
- **Dramatic function:** Complete the political judgement without pretending acknowledgement can repair death.
- **Turn:** Royal authority collapses morally and bodily while Kaṇṇaki’s loss remains unchanged.
- **Performance focus:** The king’s recognition is immediate and devastating; the queen’s response is witness, not rivalry.
- **Production requirements:** Court collapse blocking, sceptre and parasol continuity, silence and controlled crowd reaction.
- **Transition out:** Kaṇṇaki leaves royal collapse behind and addresses remembered faithful women before judging the city.
- **Interpretive limit:** Do not make royal remorse sufficient redemption or erase the institutional chain beneath the king.
