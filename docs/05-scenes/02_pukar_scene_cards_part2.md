# Pukār Scene Architecture — Part 2

## Scope

Scenes `SCN-PUK-013`–`SCN-PUK-024` carry the Kāṉal Vari misunderstanding into separation, Mādhavi’s material appeal, Kaṇṇaki’s warning and judgement, Kōvalaṉ’s return, the anklet recovery plan and the departure from Pukār into the road narrative.

## SCN-PUK-013 — Mādhavi answers in the same language

- **Canto:** 7. Kāṉal Vari
- **Location / time:** Beach pavilion; dawn continuing
- **Source:** HWT-0048
- **Characters / institutions:** Mādhavi, Kōvalaṉ, yāḻ
- **Form / lock:** MIXED_PERFORMANCE · LOCKED_SOURCE
- **Source action:** Mādhavi interprets hidden meaning in Kōvalaṉ’s song and responds through the same musical form.
- **Dramatic function:** Make counter-speech active while preserving ambiguity between performance, intention and interpretation.
- **Turn:** Artistic reciprocity becomes an interpretive contest.
- **Performance focus:** Technical control continues under hurt, pride and uncertainty.
- **Production requirements:** Continuous live-performance coverage, precise listening close-ups and musical structure that permits multiple readings.
- **Transition out:** Kōvalaṉ’s listening closes before her performance has emotionally resolved.
- **Interpretive limit:** Her song must not be treated as objective confession of infidelity.

## SCN-PUK-014 — He leaves the performance unfinished

- **Canto:** 7. Kāṉal Vari
- **Location / time:** Pavilion and shoreline; morning
- **Source:** HWT-0049
- **Characters / institutions:** Kōvalaṉ, Mādhavi, attendants at a distance
- **Form / lock:** SOURCE_DIRECT · LOCKED_SOURCE
- **Source action:** Kōvalaṉ decides Mādhavi’s performance is deceitful and leaves.
- **Dramatic function:** Make misreading—not proven betrayal—the immediate cause of separation.
- **Turn:** A shared artistic world is broken by unilateral interpretation.
- **Performance focus:** His departure is fast, proud and self-protective; she remains with the instrument and unfinished emotional action.
- **Production requirements:** Shoreline blocking, sound withdrawal, unattended pavilion objects and controlled crowd distance.
- **Transition out:** Sea sound carries into the dry heat and altered rhythms of summer.
- **Interpretive limit:** Do not stage evidence that validates Kōvalaṉ’s suspicion.

## SCN-PUK-015 — Summer after the rupture

- **Canto:** 8. Summer
- **Location / time:** Pukār interiors and wider Tamil seasonal geography; hot season
- **Source:** HWT-0050, HWT-0051
- **Characters / institutions:** Mādhavi, musicians, attendants, city under seasonal change
- **Form / lock:** SOURCE_COMPOSITE · DEVELOPMENT
- **Source action:** Summer links major Tamil cities while Mādhavi tunes, tests modes and plays the yāḻ with technical expertise.
- **Dramatic function:** Show that artistic labour continues after abandonment while the political-geographic world remains larger than the relationship.
- **Turn:** Publicly trained routine becomes a private means of surviving uncertainty.
- **Performance focus:** Repeated tuning, failed concentration and recovery without loss of skill.
- **Production requirements:** Heat, ventilation, instrument maintenance, changed light, reduced public energy and seasonal sound design.
- **Transition out:** Technical repetition yields to the deliberate crafting of a written message.
- **Interpretive limit:** Do not portray her as artistically disabled by abandonment.

## SCN-PUK-016 — The flower-letter

- **Canto:** 8. Summer
- **Location / time:** Mādhavi’s residence; late day into evening
- **Source:** HWT-0052, HWT-0053
- **Characters / institutions:** Mādhavi, Vasantamālai, writing and messenger practice
- **Form / lock:** MIXED_PERFORMANCE · LOCKED_ADAPTATION
- **Source action:** Mādhavi writes a material flower-letter and sends it through Vasantamālai, encoding her appeal through named performance forms.
- **Dramatic function:** Translate public artistic intelligence into a smaller and more vulnerable object.
- **Turn:** She moves from indirect performed answer to deliberate material communication.
- **Performance focus:** Selection, revision, scent, seal and hesitation carry more than explanatory monologue.
- **Production requirements:** Historically reviewed writing materials, flower and pigment preparation, seal logic and messenger route.
- **Transition out:** The sealed message is refused or fails to restore the relationship, leaving it to travel later through another encounter.
- **Interpretive limit:** Do not convert the letter into generic modern romantic prose.

## SCN-PUK-017 — Sacred plurality and the warning dream

- **Canto:** 9. Dream
- **Location / time:** Kaṇṇaki’s household and Pukār’s sacred environment; night
- **Source:** HWT-0054, HWT-0055
- **Characters / institutions:** Kaṇṇaki, Dēvanti, multiple shrines and sacred establishments
- **Form / lock:** MIXED_PERFORMANCE · DEVELOPMENT
- **Source action:** Kaṇṇaki’s social world includes many sacred institutions; her dream anticipates accusation, royal confrontation and danger to a city.
- **Dramatic function:** Give her an early act of interpretation without making her omniscient.
- **Turn:** Private unease becomes an articulated warning that cannot yet be acted on with certainty.
- **Performance focus:** Dream imagery should emerge from known objects, streets and institutions rather than detached fantasy symbolism.
- **Production requirements:** Reusable sacred locations, sound fragments, anklet motif and controlled dream grammar.
- **Transition out:** Dēvanti proposes a ritual response in waking space.
- **Interpretive limit:** The dream must not reveal the full plot or remove responsibility from later decisions.

## SCN-PUK-018 — Kaṇṇaki refuses the proposed remedy

- **Canto:** 9. Dream
- **Location / time:** Kaṇṇaki’s household; morning
- **Source:** HWT-0056
- **Characters / institutions:** Kaṇṇaki, Dēvanti, ritual custom
- **Form / lock:** SOURCE_DIRECT · LOCKED_SOURCE
- **Source action:** Kaṇṇaki rejects ritual bathing as an unfitting means of recovering her husband.
- **Dramatic function:** Establish judgement and ethical agency before Kōvalaṉ returns.
- **Turn:** She declines an available ritual path and remains within unresolved domestic reality.
- **Performance focus:** Quiet certainty rather than hostility toward Dēvanti or religious practice.
- **Production requirements:** Domestic ritual objects, water preparation and restrained two-person blocking.
- **Transition out:** The refused water remains unused as Kōvalaṉ enters the household.
- **Interpretive limit:** Do not turn the scene into a broad rejection of sacred traditions.

## SCN-PUK-019 — Return without restoration

- **Canto:** 9. Dream
- **Location / time:** Kaṇṇaki’s household; day
- **Source:** HWT-0057
- **Characters / institutions:** Kaṇṇaki, Kōvalaṉ, absent family wealth
- **Form / lock:** SOURCE_DIRECT · DEVELOPMENT
- **Source action:** Kōvalaṉ returns, sees Kaṇṇaki’s suffering and admits that inherited wealth has been lost.
- **Dramatic function:** Reunite them through shame and need without pretending that relational injury is repaired.
- **Turn:** He moves from avoidance to explicit acknowledgement of material collapse.
- **Performance focus:** Reduced eye contact, broken confidence, Kaṇṇaki’s measured reception and silence around what cannot be restored immediately.
- **Production requirements:** Previously established household now visibly reduced or emotionally altered; no excessive explanatory dialogue.
- **Transition out:** His admission creates the practical question of what remains.
- **Interpretive limit:** A fuller apology may be developed, but cannot be presented as directly preserved Tamil dialogue.

## SCN-PUK-020 — The anklet becomes capital

- **Canto:** 9. Dream
- **Location / time:** Household storage or private chamber; continuous
- **Source:** HWT-0058
- **Characters / institutions:** Kaṇṇaki, Kōvalaṉ, anklet pair, merchant logic
- **Form / lock:** SOURCE_DIRECT · LOCKED_SOURCE
- **Source action:** Kaṇṇaki offers the anklet and the couple decide to rebuild wealth in Madurai.
- **Dramatic function:** Convert the title object from marital property into surviving capital and future evidence.
- **Turn:** Private possession becomes the material basis for departure.
- **Performance focus:** Deliberate handling, counting, weighing and mutual recognition of dependence.
- **Production requirements:** Matched anklet pair, interior construction visible enough to support later gem-versus-pearl proof and continuity tracking.
- **Transition out:** The anklet is wrapped for travel while the household sleeps.
- **Interpretive limit:** Do not stage the offer as impulsive forgiveness detached from economic reality.

## SCN-PUK-021 — Leaving before the city wakes

- **Canto:** 10. Seeing the Country
- **Location / time:** Merchant house to long city gate; pre-dawn
- **Source:** HWT-0059
- **Characters / institutions:** Kaṇṇaki, Kōvalaṉ, sleeping household, city guards and sacred sites
- **Form / lock:** SOURCE_DIRECT · LOCKED_SOURCE
- **Source action:** They leave in the pre-dawn darkness through the long gate and pass several sacred establishments.
- **Dramatic function:** Make departure an irreversible bodily action rather than a spoken plan.
- **Turn:** They become travellers and lose immediate protection of household, kin and city status.
- **Performance focus:** Quiet packing, unfamiliar load distribution, Kaṇṇaki’s first road steps and Kōvalaṉ’s effort to appear certain.
- **Production requirements:** Night continuity, gate mechanics, portable goods, reduced costume, sleeping-city sound and safe low-light movement.
- **Transition out:** Sacred sites appear along the route toward the city edge.
- **Interpretive limit:** Do not add a secret pursuit or farewell scene unless marked as controlled bridge.

## SCN-PUK-022 — Crossing the sacred and civic edge

- **Canto:** 10. Seeing the Country
- **Location / time:** City perimeter, Kāviri approach and sacred establishments; dawn
- **Source:** HWT-0059, HWT-0060
- **Characters / institutions:** Kaṇṇaki, Kōvalaṉ, Viṣṇu-associated, Buddhist and Jain-associated sites, outer settlement
- **Form / lock:** SOURCE_COMPOSITE · RESEARCH_HOLD
- **Source action:** The pair pass plural sacred sites, leave the city edge and enter the Kāviri-side environment.
- **Dramatic function:** Preserve Pukār’s religious plurality while physically moving the couple beyond merchant protection.
- **Turn:** Civic identity gives way to exposed travel.
- **Performance focus:** They know the city signs but not yet the practical demands of the road.
- **Production requirements:** Historically differentiated sacred architecture, dawn landscape, gate-to-river geography and restraint against generic visual fusion.
- **Transition out:** Kauṇṭi observes their clothing, feet, provisions and destination.
- **Interpretive limit:** Specific architectural reconstructions remain provisional pending historical review.

## SCN-PUK-023 — Kauṇṭi assesses the travellers

- **Canto:** 10. Seeing the Country
- **Location / time:** Kāviri grove or ascetic precinct; early morning
- **Source:** HWT-0060
- **Characters / institutions:** Kauṇṭi, Kaṇṇaki, Kōvalaṉ
- **Form / lock:** SOURCE_DIRECT · LOCKED_SOURCE
- **Source action:** Kauṇṭi questions why people of their standing have left, hears Kōvalaṉ’s plan and recognises the road’s danger for Kaṇṇaki.
- **Dramatic function:** Introduce an authority grounded in ethical observation rather than wealth or marital status.
- **Turn:** The couple’s private plan is tested by an experienced outsider.
- **Performance focus:** Kauṇṭi studies feet, load and readiness; Kōvalaṉ states purpose; Kaṇṇaki’s body supplies evidence.
- **Production requirements:** Ascetic implements with practical use, grove environment and three-person spatial dynamics.
- **Transition out:** Kauṇṭi chooses to accompany them as the route opens southward.
- **Interpretive limit:** She must not recognise the full future or function as omniscient oracle.

## SCN-PUK-024 — Pukār becomes memory

- **Canto:** 10. Seeing the Country
- **Location / time:** Southward path beyond the city; morning
- **Source:** HWT-0059, HWT-0060, HWT-0061
- **Characters / institutions:** Kaṇṇaki, Kōvalaṉ, Kauṇṭi, distant Pukār
- **Form / lock:** CONTROLLED_BRIDGE · LOCKED_ADAPTATION
- **Source action:** The travellers leave Pukār, meet Kauṇṭi and enter a route where orchard and field hazards immediately demand care.
- **Dramatic function:** Close the city movement and make the first physical difficulty announce the road’s new terms.
- **Turn:** Pukār changes from present environment to a home that can only be recalled.
- **Performance focus:** Kaṇṇaki adjusts her step; Kauṇṭi intervenes practically; Kōvalaṉ’s confidence meets terrain.
- **Production requirements:** Viewline or sound memory of Pukār, orchard hazards, travel continuity and the first visible foot strain.
- **Transition out:** Begins the Road Scene Architecture at `SCN-ROAD-001`.
- **Interpretive limit:** The exact final view of Pukār is cinematic interpretation; no invented farewell speech should be attributed to the epic.
