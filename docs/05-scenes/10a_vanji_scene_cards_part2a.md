# Vañci Scene Architecture — Part 2A

## Scope

Scenes `SCN-VAN-011`–`SCN-VAN-015` move from royal reflection to the memorial proposal, stone debate, northern vow and the administrative preparation of the expedition.

## SCN-VAN-011 — Rule is described as fear and burden
- **Canto:** 25. Vision
- **Location / time:** Royal hill audience; after Sāttanār’s account
- **Source:** HWT-0166
- **Characters / institutions:** Senguttuvan, queen, court and remembered rulers
- **Form / lock:** SOURCE_DIRECT · LOCKED_SOURCE
- **Source action:** Senguttuvan reflects that rulers fear failed rain, unjust death, oppressive rule and the burden of protecting subjects.
- **Dramatic function:** Make the response begin with political accountability rather than military ambition.
- **Turn:** The Madurai story becomes a warning about kingship within the Cēra court.
- **Performance focus:** Reflection is addressed to people who can affect policy, not delivered as abstract philosophy.
- **Production requirements:** Court stillness, restrained royal objects and continuity from testimony.
- **Transition out:** Iḷaṅkō Vēṇmāḷ proposes a concrete memorial response.
- **Interpretive limit:** Do not treat the speech as proof that Cēra rule is flawless.

## SCN-VAN-012 — The queen turns grief into policy
- **Canto:** 25. Vision
- **Location / time:** Royal hill audience; continuous
- **Source:** HWT-0167
- **Characters / institutions:** Iḷaṅkō Vēṇmāḷ, Senguttuvan, court
- **Form / lock:** SOURCE_DIRECT · LOCKED_SOURCE
- **Source action:** Iḷaṅkō Vēṇmāḷ recommends honouring the Pattini who reached Cēra country.
- **Dramatic function:** Give the memorial project an explicit ethical proposal before the king enlarges it politically.
- **Turn:** Testimony becomes a state decision under consideration.
- **Performance focus:** The queen distinguishes honouring Kaṇṇaki from merely mourning another kingdom’s disaster.
- **Production requirements:** Clear council hierarchy and reaction from specialists who must implement the proposal.
- **Transition out:** Court experts debate the stone and consecration system.
- **Interpretive limit:** Do not reduce the queen to ceremonial assent or inflate her beyond the preserved decision.

## SCN-VAN-013 — A monument begins as a material argument
- **Canto:** 25. Vision
- **Location / time:** Court consultation; continuous
- **Source:** HWT-0168
- **Characters / institutions:** Senguttuvan, queen, ritual and material specialists
- **Form / lock:** SOURCE_COMPOSITE · RESEARCH_HOLD
- **Source action:** Specialists compare southern stone and Kāviri consecration with Himalayan stone and Ganges consecration for the Pattini image.
- **Dramatic function:** Show that monumentalisation requires choices about material, geography, ritual authority and scale.
- **Turn:** A local memorial option becomes a debate about transregional prestige.
- **Performance focus:** Experts disagree through practical consequences—distance, ritual sequence, stone quality and political meaning.
- **Production requirements:** Stone samples, route maps, consecration vocabulary and specialist consultation.
- **Transition out:** Senguttuvan chooses the northern path and frames it as a royal vow.
- **Interpretive limit:** Do not imply the Himalayan option is inherently more sacred or erase the viable southern alternative.

## SCN-VAN-014 — Memorial decision becomes northern vow
- **Canto:** 25. Vision
- **Location / time:** Cēra court and proclamation space; day
- **Source:** HWT-0169, HWT-0170
- **Characters / institutions:** Senguttuvan, ministers, heralds, foreign spies and northern rulers as intended audience
- **Form / lock:** SOURCE_COMPOSITE · LOCKED_ADAPTATION
- **Source action:** Senguttuvan turns the memorial decision into a northern expedition and ministers use public proclamation and known foreign intelligence networks to transmit the demand.
- **Dramatic function:** Expose how commemoration becomes intertwined with sovereignty, prestige and coercive communication.
- **Turn:** The Pattini project acquires military and international consequences.
- **Performance focus:** The vow is public, strategic and heard by audiences beyond the visible court.
- **Production requirements:** Proclamation drums, herald routes, spy-network staging and political map logic.
- **Transition out:** Court professionals identify the auspicious moment and mobilise the state.
- **Interpretive limit:** Do not present the expedition as the only possible way to honour Kaṇṇaki.

## SCN-VAN-015 — The state prepares bodies, time and food
- **Canto:** 26. Taking the Stone
- **Location / time:** Vañci administrative and military spaces; preparation days
- **Source:** HWT-0171, HWT-0172, HWT-0173
- **Characters / institutions:** Court expert, five great councils, eight bodies, accountants, strategists, army leadership and provisioners
- **Form / lock:** SOURCE_COMPOSITE · RESEARCH_HOLD
- **Source action:** An auspicious moment is chosen; sword and parasol turn north; councils, administrative specialists and food provision organise the expedition.
- **Dramatic function:** Make mobilisation an institutional labour system rather than one king’s gesture.
- **Turn:** Public vow becomes scheduled, provisioned and administratively executable movement.
- **Performance focus:** Counting grain, assigning responsibility, recording units and resolving practical conflict.
- **Production requirements:** Administrative tablets or documents, stores, kitchens, military registers and calendrical consultation.
- **Transition out:** Sacred and public departure rites place the mobilised state on the road.
- **Interpretive limit:** Exact reconstruction of named councils and offices remains under historical review.
