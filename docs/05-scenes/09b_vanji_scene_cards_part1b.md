# Vañci Scene Architecture — Part 1B

## Scope

Scenes `SCN-VAN-006`–`SCN-VAN-010` move from local Pattini praise to the Cēra hill excursion, ecological tribute, Kuravar eyewitness testimony and Sāttanār’s transmission of the Madurai catastrophe.

## SCN-VAN-006 — The hill song judges Madurai
- **Canto:** 24. Hill Kuravai
- **Location / time:** Hill settlement ritual; continuing
- **Source:** HWT-0159
- **Characters / institutions:** Hill women, Kaṇṇaki as Pattini, remembered Madurai
- **Form / lock:** MIXED_PERFORMANCE · LOCKED_SOURCE
- **Source action:** The women convert the destruction of unjust Madurai into a communal Pattini praise song.
- **Dramatic function:** Complete the first local interpretation of Kaṇṇaki before royal testimony begins.
- **Turn:** A stranger’s grief becomes a community’s moral song.
- **Performance focus:** Praise retains the violence and injustice that produced it; it is not serene devotional closure.
- **Production requirements:** Kuravai continuation, recurring anklet rhythm and community-scale ritual space.
- **Transition out:** Mountain song carries into the Cēra ruler’s excursion through the same landscape.
- **Interpretive limit:** Do not make the song a state anthem or later institutional liturgy.

## SCN-VAN-007 — The court enters mountain country
- **Canto:** 25. Vision
- **Location / time:** From Vañci’s royal complex into the hills; day
- **Source:** HWT-0160, HWT-0161
- **Characters / institutions:** Senguttuvan, Iḷaṅkō Vēṇmāḷ, retinue, hill workers and performers
- **Form / lock:** SOURCE_COMPOSITE · LOCKED_ADAPTATION
- **Source action:** The ruler and queen leave the Vañci complex to view mountain country amid kuravai, field calls, drums, waterfalls, elephant keepers, hunters and army movement.
- **Dramatic function:** Bring royal power into an already sounding and inhabited landscape.
- **Turn:** The court becomes a listener and receiver rather than the origin of the hill world.
- **Performance focus:** The retinue must negotiate local labour and sound instead of visually possessing the landscape.
- **Production requirements:** Processional scale, layered hill soundscape, elephant-management research and terrain logistics.
- **Transition out:** Hill communities approach with tribute goods and specialised knowledge.
- **Interpretive limit:** Do not film the excursion as discovery of an empty frontier.

## SCN-VAN-008 — Tribute carries ecological knowledge
- **Canto:** 25. Vision
- **Location / time:** Royal hill camp or audience space; day
- **Source:** HWT-0162, HWT-0163
- **Characters / institutions:** Hill communities, court, forest specialists, animals and produce
- **Form / lock:** SOURCE_COMPOSITE · RESEARCH_HOLD
- **Source action:** Ivory, eaglewood, honey, sandal, pigments, spices, fruit, sugarcane, cultivated produce, young animals and birds are presented as tribute.
- **Dramatic function:** Show tribute as a political transfer of material wealth and ecological access, not picturesque gifting.
- **Turn:** The court receives evidence of the hill country’s labour, extraction and specialised knowledge.
- **Performance focus:** Weight, handling, animal stress and the formality of presentation remain visible.
- **Production requirements:** Ethical animal alternatives, botanical research, trade containers, tribute accounting and court protocol.
- **Transition out:** Material tribute is followed by testimony that cannot be reduced to an object.
- **Interpretive limit:** Do not celebrate extraction without showing labour and asymmetry.

## SCN-VAN-009 — The Kuravar report what they saw
- **Canto:** 25. Vision
- **Location / time:** Royal hill audience; continuous
- **Source:** HWT-0164
- **Characters / institutions:** Kuravar witnesses, Senguttuvan, Iḷaṅkō Vēṇmāḷ, court
- **Form / lock:** SOURCE_DIRECT · LOCKED_SOURCE
- **Source action:** The Kuravar report seeing the solitary one-breasted woman beneath the vēṅkai tree ascend with her husband.
- **Dramatic function:** Establish local eyewitness authority before learned or royal synthesis.
- **Turn:** The tribute audience becomes an inquiry into a recent extraordinary event.
- **Performance focus:** Witnesses distinguish what they saw from what they do not know.
- **Production requirements:** Court listening geometry, restrained witness replay and continuity with `SCN-VAN-001`.
- **Transition out:** Sāttanār identifies the woman by connecting the report to the anklet case.
- **Interpretive limit:** Do not make the Kuravar merely confirm a story the court already possesses.

## SCN-VAN-010 — Sāttanār connects witness to history
- **Canto:** 25. Vision
- **Location / time:** Royal hill audience; continuous
- **Source:** HWT-0165
- **Characters / institutions:** Sāttanār, Kuravar witnesses, Senguttuvan, queen and court
- **Form / lock:** SOURCE_DIRECT · LOCKED_SOURCE
- **Source action:** Sāttanār narrates the anklet case, the burning of Madurai and the deaths of the Pāṇḍya king and queen.
- **Dramatic function:** Convert separate eyewitness and southern reports into a political account without erasing their different sources.
- **Turn:** The Cēra court understands that the hill encounter belongs to a completed justice catastrophe.
- **Performance focus:** Sāttanār marks reported events, direct knowledge and causal sequence rather than speaking as omniscient author.
- **Production requirements:** Testimony structure, brief source-memory images and court reaction continuity.
- **Transition out:** Senguttuvan reflects on the burden and fear of rule.
- **Interpretive limit:** Do not merge the character-witness automatically with every later authorial tradition.
