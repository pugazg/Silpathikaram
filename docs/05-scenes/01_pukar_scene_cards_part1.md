# Pukār Scene Architecture — Part 1

## Scope

Scenes `SCN-PUK-001`–`SCN-PUK-012` establish the epic’s moral contract, Pukār’s merchant and performance institutions, the marriage, Mādhavi’s artistic world, the festival city and the opening of the Kāṉal Vari rupture.

## SCN-PUK-001 — Moral contract

- **Canto:** Prologue
- **Location / time:** Formal epic space; outside ordinary chronology
- **Source:** HWT-0001
- **Characters / institutions:** Kaṇṇaki, Kōvalaṉ, future public memory
- **Form / lock:** MIXED_PERFORMANCE · LOCKED_SOURCE
- **Source action:** Political failure, Pattini recognition, causation and the anklet are declared as the work’s governing design.
- **Dramatic function:** Give the audience a formal promise without revealing every later event.
- **Turn:** The anklet is established as property, evidence and title-bearing object rather than ornament alone.
- **Performance focus:** Voice, inscription, ritual recitation or object-led montage rather than explanatory dialogue.
- **Production requirements:** Anklet macro-detail; three-world image vocabulary; title design; recurring sound motif.
- **Transition out:** Anklet sound resolves into wedding percussion and the waking abundance of Pukār.
- **Interpretive limit:** Do not present a complete prophecy that removes uncertainty from later choices.

## SCN-PUK-002 — The city that can be lost

- **Canto:** 1. Wedding Blessing; 2. Domestic Virtue
- **Location / time:** Kāviri-fed Pukār; morning into working day
- **Source:** HWT-0029, HWT-0033
- **Characters / institutions:** Port labour, merchants, households, carriers, ships
- **Form / lock:** SOURCE_COMPOSITE · LOCKED_ADAPTATION
- **Source action:** Pukār is praised through moon, sun, rain, Kāviri abundance, ships and land-borne goods.
- **Dramatic function:** Establish a functioning maritime city before any romantic or moral rupture.
- **Turn:** The city becomes a causal system that supports the principal families’ confidence and wealth.
- **Performance focus:** Labour and exchange lead; principal characters are initially small within the city.
- **Production requirements:** Harbour logistics, warehouses, carts, wet goods, textiles, animals, water management and crowd layers.
- **Transition out:** Goods move from port to the two merchant houses preparing for marriage.
- **Interpretive limit:** Do not reduce Pukār to a fantasy palace or generic ancient marketplace.

## SCN-PUK-003 — Two merchant houses make a marriage

- **Canto:** 1. Wedding Blessing
- **Location / time:** Merchant quarters and public wedding route; day
- **Source:** HWT-0030, HWT-0031, HWT-0032
- **Characters / institutions:** Kaṇṇaki, Kōvalaṉ, both families, women on elephants, ritual and civic participants
- **Form / lock:** SOURCE_DIRECT · LOCKED_SOURCE
- **Source action:** Two wealthy merchant lineages join through a publicly displayed marriage using elephants, drums, conches, lamps and a pavilion.
- **Dramatic function:** Establish kinship, property, social expectation and the scale of what later choices endanger.
- **Turn:** Kaṇṇaki and Kōvalaṉ become a recognised household pair under public witness.
- **Performance focus:** Their private reactions remain contained within choreographed family and civic ritual.
- **Production requirements:** Wedding pavilion, elephants, lamps, textiles, percussion, conches, merchant insignia and layered guests.
- **Transition out:** Public celebration contracts into the practical work of a new household.
- **Interpretive limit:** Do not invent a modern courtship history as textual fact.

## SCN-PUK-004 — A household of their own

- **Canto:** 2. Domestic Virtue
- **Location / time:** Multi-level merchant residence; changing seasons
- **Source:** HWT-0034, HWT-0035
- **Characters / institutions:** Kaṇṇaki, Kōvalaṉ, servants, kin, guests
- **Form / lock:** SOURCE_COMPOSITE · DEVELOPMENT
- **Source action:** Upper rooms, windows, terraces, floral interiors and hospitality duties define the couple’s independent domestic sphere.
- **Dramatic function:** Give Kaṇṇaki visible competence and establish the material life from which Kōvalaṉ later departs.
- **Turn:** Marriage becomes daily responsibility rather than ceremonial image.
- **Performance focus:** Kaṇṇaki handles stores, guests and household timing; Kōvalaṉ moves with inherited ease.
- **Production requirements:** Vertical domestic architecture, ledgers, storage, guest service, lamps, textiles and seasonal airflow.
- **Transition out:** Household music from afar draws attention toward the city’s performance institutions.
- **Interpretive limit:** Domestic competence may be dramatised, but invented conflicts must not be claimed as source events.

## SCN-PUK-005 — Seven years in the body

- **Canto:** 3. Arangēṟṟu
- **Location / time:** Training rooms and rehearsal spaces; multi-year progression
- **Source:** HWT-0036, HWT-0037
- **Characters / institutions:** Mādhavi, dance teacher, music and poetry specialists, ensemble trainees
- **Form / lock:** SOURCE_COMPOSITE · LOCKED_ADAPTATION
- **Source action:** Mādhavi trains for seven years with specialist teachers in dance, song, yāḻ, flute, percussion and poetic form.
- **Dramatic function:** Establish mastery and institutional labour before romance or public glamour.
- **Turn:** A child trainee becomes a performer capable of royal evaluation.
- **Performance focus:** Repetition, correction, breath, feet, listening and exact recovery from error.
- **Production requirements:** Historically reviewed training costume, instruments, floor preparation, notation practices and teacher hierarchy.
- **Transition out:** A measured rehearsal line becomes the measured line used to construct the public stage.
- **Interpretive limit:** Do not present her talent as spontaneous sensuality or supernatural gift.

## SCN-PUK-006 — Building and consecrating the stage

- **Canto:** 3. Arangēṟṟu
- **Location / time:** Performance arena; days before debut
- **Source:** HWT-0038, HWT-0039, HWT-0040
- **Characters / institutions:** Stage workers, ritual specialists, musicians, court representatives, Mādhavi
- **Form / lock:** MIXED_PERFORMANCE · RESEARCH_HOLD
- **Source action:** The stage is measured, fitted with entrances, lamps, curtains and canopy, ritually inaugurated and organised around a formal ensemble sequence.
- **Dramatic function:** Show that performance is civic infrastructure and coordinated knowledge.
- **Turn:** Private training enters a space where the city and court can judge it.
- **Performance focus:** Mādhavi studies the stage while others test sound, sightlines and ritual order.
- **Production requirements:** Stage engineering, curtains, canopy, sacred headstaff, instruments, oil-lamp safety and audience geometry.
- **Transition out:** The last tuning sound opens into the debut before the assembled court.
- **Interpretive limit:** Material reconstruction remains on research hold; avoid importing later theatre architecture without evidence.

## SCN-PUK-007 — Debut, reward and the garland threshold

- **Canto:** 3. Arangēṟṟu
- **Location / time:** Royal performance arena and public access point; evening
- **Source:** HWT-0041
- **Characters / institutions:** Mādhavi, ruler, ensemble, brokers, audience, Kōvalaṉ
- **Form / lock:** SOURCE_DIRECT · DEVELOPMENT
- **Source action:** Royal honour, payment and the sale of Mādhavi’s garland connect artistic prestige with patronage and personal access.
- **Dramatic function:** Bring Kōvalaṉ into Mādhavi’s world through a choice shaped by art, wealth and public economy.
- **Turn:** Public admiration becomes a private relational threshold when he acquires the garland.
- **Performance focus:** Mādhavi receives honour as a professional; Kōvalaṉ chooses rather than being passively enchanted.
- **Production requirements:** Full ensemble, royal reward objects, headstaff, garland, brokered transaction and crowd response.
- **Transition out:** The garland passes from public display into an intimate interior.
- **Interpretive limit:** No single modern label should be used as though it fully defines Mādhavi’s social position.

## SCN-PUK-008 — Two evenings in one city

- **Canto:** 4. Evening
- **Location / time:** Mādhavi’s residence and Kaṇṇaki’s household; intercut evening
- **Source:** HWT-0042, HWT-0043
- **Characters / institutions:** Mādhavi, Kōvalaṉ, Kaṇṇaki, attendants
- **Form / lock:** PARALLEL_SOURCE · LOCKED_ADAPTATION
- **Source action:** Lamps, flowers and music mark evening while Mādhavi prepares for union and Kaṇṇaki removes ornament in separation.
- **Dramatic function:** Show two forms of labour and expectation without inventing a face-to-face rivalry.
- **Turn:** The same city evening creates intimacy in one household and absence in another.
- **Performance focus:** Preparation and subtraction carry emotion more strongly than explanatory speech.
- **Production requirements:** Matched lamp cues, contrasting ornament handling, domestic sound bleed and precise parallel editing.
- **Transition out:** Private lamps multiply into the lights of the festival city.
- **Interpretive limit:** Parallel structure must not imply Kaṇṇaki knows details the text does not give her.

## SCN-PUK-009 — The festival city mobilises

- **Canto:** 5. Indra Festival
- **Location / time:** Maruvūrppākkam, Paṭṭiṉappākkam and Nāḷaṅkāṭi; festival days
- **Source:** HWT-0002–HWT-0022
- **Characters / institutions:** Migrants, traders, craftspeople, performers, warriors, sacred establishments, councils and crowds
- **Form / lock:** SOURCE_COMPOSITE · RESEARCH_HOLD
- **Source action:** Pukār’s quarters, markets, workshops, civic forums, ritual protection, sacred plurality and continuous public celebration become visible.
- **Dramatic function:** Place the principal relationships inside a cosmopolitan civic machine rather than an isolated romance.
- **Turn:** The city’s normal systems transform into an intensified public festival order.
- **Performance focus:** Workers, sellers, musicians, guards and ritual participants each perform specialised actions.
- **Production requirements:** Two-quarter city plan, day market, five forums, profession-specific streets, flags, drums, elephants and crowd control.
- **Transition out:** Public spectacle narrows onto Mādhavi’s codified dance repertoire.
- **Interpretive limit:** Do not collapse the festival into an undifferentiated later-period religious spectacle; historical and ritual depiction remains under review.

## SCN-PUK-010 — Eleven dances and the working costume

- **Canto:** 6. Sea-Bathing Festival
- **Location / time:** Performance preparation and public display; festival night
- **Source:** HWT-0023, HWT-0024
- **Characters / institutions:** Mādhavi, ensemble, costume attendants, spectators
- **Form / lock:** MIXED_PERFORMANCE · RESEARCH_HOLD
- **Source action:** Eleven named dances and an exact bathing, fragrance, clothing and ornament sequence constitute Mādhavi’s repertoire and preparation.
- **Dramatic function:** Make costume, music and dance part of narrative labour rather than decorative montage.
- **Turn:** The public performer is rebuilt through disciplined preparation for another social space.
- **Performance focus:** Every ornament has weight, order and functional consequence for movement.
- **Production requirements:** Dance reconstruction, instrument research, ornament prototypes, bathing materials and movement-safe costume engineering.
- **Transition out:** The last fastening gives way to wheels and footsteps moving toward the shore.
- **Interpretive limit:** Specific choreography not recoverable from the text must be labelled as reconstruction.

## SCN-PUK-011 — Through the lit city to the sea

- **Canto:** 6. Sea-Bathing Festival
- **Location / time:** Decorated streets to the shoreline; pre-dawn
- **Source:** HWT-0025, HWT-0026, HWT-0027
- **Characters / institutions:** Mādhavi, Kōvalaṉ, sellers, boat crews, guards, elites and festival crowds
- **Form / lock:** SOURCE_COMPOSITE · LOCKED_ADAPTATION
- **Source action:** The pair move through streets where profession-specific lamps identify sellers, boats, lighthouse, guards and the crowded coastal assembly.
- **Dramatic function:** Let the journey to intimacy pass through visible labour, trade and public access.
- **Turn:** Urban enclosure opens into a vast communal shore.
- **Performance focus:** Kōvalaṉ and Mādhavi move together but are continually observed and interrupted by city life.
- **Production requirements:** Night street extension, oil lamps by profession, lighthouse, boats, goods, beach crowd and controlled dawn light.
- **Transition out:** Crowd noise falls away beneath a temporary pavilion by the sea.
- **Interpretive limit:** The route may be condensed, but profession-specific lighting must not become generic decoration.

## SCN-PUK-012 — The song that begins the break

- **Canto:** 6. Sea-Bathing Festival; 7. Kāṉal Vari
- **Location / time:** Painted pavilion beneath the puṉṉai tree; dawn
- **Source:** HWT-0028, HWT-0045, HWT-0046, HWT-0047
- **Characters / institutions:** Kōvalaṉ, Mādhavi, yāḻ, fisher and coastal world
- **Form / lock:** MIXED_PERFORMANCE · LOCKED_SOURCE
- **Source action:** Within temporary privacy Kōvalaṉ handles the yāḻ through named techniques and sings of Kāviri, kingship and the coastal fisher world.
- **Dramatic function:** Begin the relationship rupture through accomplished but indirect artistic speech.
- **Turn:** A shared musical language acquires meanings the two may no longer share.
- **Performance focus:** Kōvalaṉ’s technique is credible; Mādhavi listens for implications beneath display.
- **Production requirements:** Beach pavilion, yāḻ specialist, coastal labour references, live sound strategy and dawn-sea continuity.
- **Transition out:** His final phrase is held in Mādhavi’s listening before she takes the instrument to answer.
- **Interpretive limit:** The song may carry ambiguity; it must not be rewritten into an explicit modern accusation.
