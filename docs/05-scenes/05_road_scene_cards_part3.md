# Road Scene Architecture — Part 3

## Scope

Scenes `SCN-ROAD-013`–`SCN-ROAD-018` move the travellers through night passage, outer refuge, messenger correction and the sensory threshold of Madurai.

## SCN-ROAD-013 — Moonlight under the sceptre

- **Canto:** 13. Halt in the Outer Settlement
- **Location / time:** Summer road in Pāṇḍya territory; night
- **Source:** HWT-0081
- **Characters / institutions:** Kaṇṇaki, Kōvalaṉ, Kauṇṭi, remembered Pāṇḍya order
- **Form / lock:** SOURCE_DIRECT · DEVELOPMENT
- **Source action:** The group crosses the dangerous summer region by moonlight; the poem attributes unusual safety to just Pāṇḍya rule.
- **Dramatic function:** Establish the expectation of reliable kingship before Madurai violates it.
- **Turn:** The capital’s justice is trusted before any character enters its institutions.
- **Performance focus:** Quiet night discipline and temporary relief from heat.
- **Production requirements:** Moonlit route, sparse animal danger, travel lamps and sound restraint.
- **Transition out:** The road leads to a settlement associated with learned religious householders.
- **Interpretive limit:** The source’s praise of order must not be treated as objective guarantee that every subject is safe.

## SCN-ROAD-014 — Shelter outside the capital

- **Canto:** 13. Halt in the Outer Settlement
- **Location / time:** Outer religious settlement; late night into morning
- **Source:** HWT-0082
- **Characters / institutions:** Kaṇṇaki, Kōvalaṉ, Kauṇṭi, learned householders
- **Form / lock:** SOURCE_DIRECT · LOCKED_SOURCE
- **Source action:** The travellers reach a protected outer settlement and Kaṇṇaki is lodged with Kauṇṭi.
- **Dramatic function:** Give one last layer of social protection before entry into the city’s risk system.
- **Turn:** Kōvalaṉ can move outward to gather information while Kaṇṇaki remains sheltered.
- **Performance focus:** Practical placement, food, water and guarded rest rather than sentimental welcome.
- **Production requirements:** Outer-settlement architecture, sleeping arrangements, modest household activity and dawn continuity.
- **Transition out:** Kōvalaṉ encounters a traveller who recognises him.
- **Interpretive limit:** Do not merge this temporary refuge with Mādhari’s later pastoral household.

## SCN-ROAD-015 — Kausika recognises the missing man

- **Canto:** 13. Halt in the Outer Settlement
- **Location / time:** Outside the settlement; morning
- **Source:** HWT-0083
- **Characters:** Kōvalaṉ, Kausika
- **Form / lock:** SOURCE_DIRECT · LOCKED_SOURCE
- **Source action:** Kausika recognises Kōvalaṉ through carefully framed speech referring to Mādhavi.
- **Dramatic function:** Let Pukār’s social network catch up with the traveller before Madurai absorbs him.
- **Turn:** Kōvalaṉ can no longer treat departure as a private break without consequences elsewhere.
- **Performance focus:** Testing recognition, guarded response and the shock of hearing a former life named on the road.
- **Production requirements:** Two-person roadside blocking, sealed message continuity and restrained background movement.
- **Transition out:** Kausika reports the grief and search left behind.
- **Interpretive limit:** Do not stage him as an official pursuer or agent of punishment.

## SCN-ROAD-016 — The letter corrects the song

- **Canto:** 13. Halt in the Outer Settlement
- **Location / time:** Roadside or outer lodging; continuous
- **Source:** HWT-0084, HWT-0085
- **Characters:** Kōvalaṉ, Kausika, remembered Mādhavi and merchant families
- **Form / lock:** SOURCE_COMPOSITE · LOCKED_SOURCE
- **Source action:** Kausika reports the families’ grief and Mādhavi’s collapse; the scented, sealed letter leads Kōvalaṉ to recognise her innocence and his own fault.
- **Dramatic function:** Correct the Kāṉal Vari misreading before Kōvalaṉ dies.
- **Turn:** His certainty about Mādhavi collapses, but there is no route back to repair what has happened.
- **Performance focus:** Material recognition through scent, seal and handwriting before spoken admission.
- **Production requirements:** Exact flower-letter continuity, memory sound from Pukār and close handling of the object.
- **Transition out:** He sends the message toward his parents while Madurai announces itself through air and sound.
- **Interpretive limit:** Recognition of Mādhavi’s innocence does not complete reconciliation with either woman.

## SCN-ROAD-017 — Madurai arrives before it is seen

- **Canto:** 13. Halt in the Outer Settlement
- **Location / time:** Outer precincts before dawn
- **Source:** HWT-0086, HWT-0087
- **Characters / institutions:** Travellers, bards, cooks, sellers, temples, palace, ascetics, animals
- **Form / lock:** MIXED_PERFORMANCE · LOCKED_ADAPTATION
- **Source action:** Music, sandal, flowers, kitchens, sweet shops, smoke, temple and palace drums, recitation, elephants and horses announce the city before its walls are reached.
- **Dramatic function:** Build Madurai as an institutional presence through sensory evidence.
- **Turn:** The hoped-for market becomes a vast capital whose systems exceed the travellers’ knowledge.
- **Performance focus:** The three travellers interpret different signals: commerce, danger, shelter and authority.
- **Production requirements:** Layered off-screen sound, scent cues, smoke, distant architecture and animal calls.
- **Transition out:** The Vaigai appears as the final natural and symbolic boundary.
- **Interpretive limit:** Do not compress all sounds into one generic religious dawn montage.

## SCN-ROAD-018 — Across the Vaigai to the fortified edge

- **Canto:** 13. Halt in the Outer Settlement
- **Location / time:** Vaigai banks, raft crossing, moat and outer precincts; dawn
- **Source:** HWT-0088, HWT-0089
- **Characters / institutions:** Kaṇṇaki, Kōvalaṉ, Kauṇṭi, raft workers, guards, cultivated outer precincts
- **Form / lock:** MIXED_PERFORMANCE · LOCKED_SOURCE
- **Source action:** The Vaigai is personified through flowering banks and concealed grief; the travellers cross by raft, circle moat and rampart, read ominous banners and enter the cultivated edge.
- **Dramatic function:** End the road with a threshold that is beautiful, organised and morally ominous.
- **Turn:** The travellers enter the jurisdiction whose failure will determine their lives.
- **Performance focus:** Kaṇṇaki’s fatigue, Kōvalaṉ’s renewed commercial hope and Kauṇṭi’s watchfulness coexist.
- **Production requirements:** Vaigai ecology, raft mechanics, walls, moat, banners, fields and dawn crowd transition.
- **Transition out:** Begins the Madurai Scene Architecture at `SCN-MDU-001`.
- **Interpretive limit:** Omen imagery must not imply that the characters knowingly enter a predetermined death trap.
