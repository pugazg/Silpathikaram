# Madurai Scene Architecture — Part 1

## Scope

Scenes `SCN-MDU-001`–`SCN-MDU-010` move from an ordered capital and temporary pastoral refuge to Kōvalaṉ’s uninvestigated execution and the death-news reaching Kaṇṇaki.

## SCN-MDU-001 — The capital wakes behind its walls
- **Canto:** 14. Seeing the City
- **Location / time:** Outer gardens, moat, gates and urban axis; dawn
- **Source:** HWT-0090, HWT-0091, HWT-0092, HWT-0093
- **Characters / institutions:** Travellers, guards, sacred institutions, royal city services
- **Form / lock:** MIXED_PERFORMANCE · LOCKED_SOURCE
- **Source action:** Gardens, fields, sacred sites, drums, moat, walls and guarded gates reveal Madurai as an organised capital.
- **Dramatic function:** Establish institutional competence before the justice failure.
- **Turn:** The travellers cross from exposed road into a city that appears capable of order.
- **Performance focus:** Kauṇṭi reads risk; Kōvalaṉ reads opportunity; Kaṇṇaki reads scale through bodily fatigue.
- **Production requirements:** Fortified edge, gate traffic, dawn sound layers, sacred plurality and outer agriculture.
- **Transition out:** The fortified axis opens into riverfront and market circulation.
- **Interpretive limit:** Do not foreshadow the city as visibly corrupt from its first appearance.

## SCN-MDU-002 — River, market and performance city
- **Canto:** 14. Seeing the City
- **Location / time:** Vaigai riverfront and specialised streets; morning
- **Source:** HWT-0094, HWT-0095, HWT-0096, HWT-0097
- **Characters / institutions:** Merchants, performers, craft workers, river users, travellers
- **Form / lock:** SOURCE_COMPOSITE · RESEARCH_HOLD
- **Source action:** River leisure, merchant institutions, artistic labour and classified markets form a dense urban economy.
- **Dramatic function:** Make Kōvalaṉ’s plan to sell an anklet economically plausible while showing the complexity he does not know.
- **Turn:** Madurai becomes a market of opportunity and unequal local knowledge.
- **Performance focus:** Kōvalaṉ studies value and access; Kaṇṇaki remains outside the commercial movement.
- **Production requirements:** Vaigai activity, commodity streets, performance labour, weights, ledgers and craft-specific geography.
- **Transition out:** Kauṇṭi seeks safer household protection for Kaṇṇaki.
- **Interpretive limit:** Exact street reconstruction remains provisional and must not copy Pukār’s layout.

## SCN-MDU-003 — Care passes into Mādhari’s house
- **Canto:** 15. Refuge
- **Location / time:** Outer settlement and pastoral household; day
- **Source:** HWT-0098, HWT-0099
- **Characters / institutions:** Kauṇṭi, Kaṇṇaki, Kōvalaṉ, Mādhari, pastoral household
- **Form / lock:** SOURCE_DIRECT · LOCKED_SOURCE
- **Source action:** Kauṇṭi secures protection and entrusts Kaṇṇaki to Mādhari’s household hospitality.
- **Dramatic function:** Give Kaṇṇaki one final interval of food, women’s care and domestic safety.
- **Turn:** Ascetic route protection becomes local household protection.
- **Performance focus:** Practical handover of water, food, sleeping space and responsibility.
- **Production requirements:** Pastoral dwelling, cattle economy, cooking, washing and distinct household material culture.
- **Transition out:** Unease appears first through the cattle and dairy world.
- **Interpretive limit:** Do not portray the refuge as permanent security or erase Kauṇṭi’s continuing responsibility.

## SCN-MDU-004 — The cattle register disorder
- **Canto:** 17. Aycciyar Kuravai
- **Location / time:** Mādhari’s pastoral settlement; later day
- **Source:** HWT-0108
- **Characters / institutions:** Mādhari, pastoral women, calves, cattle and dairy labour
- **Form / lock:** SOURCE_DIRECT · LOCKED_SOURCE
- **Source action:** Milk, butter, calves, cattle and bells behave abnormally before human news arrives.
- **Dramatic function:** Let a working household perceive disturbance through its own material systems.
- **Turn:** Refuge becomes unsettled before anyone knows why.
- **Performance focus:** Experienced workers test ordinary processes and recognise that several failures coincide.
- **Production requirements:** Dairy continuity, animal handling, bells, vessels and practical effects.
- **Transition out:** Mādhari organises a communal performance response.
- **Interpretive limit:** Do not turn the omens into a complete supernatural explanation of Kōvalaṉ’s fate.

## SCN-MDU-005 — Kuravai as communal repair
- **Canto:** 17. Aycciyar Kuravai
- **Location / time:** Pastoral ritual space; evening
- **Source:** HWT-0109, HWT-0110, HWT-0111, HWT-0112, HWT-0113
- **Characters / institutions:** Mādhari, seven young women, pastoral community, musicians
- **Form / lock:** MIXED_PERFORMANCE · RESEARCH_HOLD
- **Source action:** Mādhari assigns musical and dramatic identities and organises a kuravai to relieve communal distress; the performance invokes sacred and political worlds.
- **Dramatic function:** Show song and dance acting upon shared fear rather than decorating the plot.
- **Turn:** The community converts unease into coordinated ritual action.
- **Performance focus:** Role assignment, rhythm, group trust and the effort to restore ordinary life.
- **Production requirements:** Kuravai research, seven-role system, vocal and instrumental design, cattle-safe staging.
- **Transition out:** The scene intercuts or resolves toward Kōvalaṉ entering the artisan market.
- **Interpretive limit:** Choreography is reconstruction and must remain labelled as such.

## SCN-MDU-006 — Kōvalaṉ carries the surviving capital alone
- **Canto:** 16. Execution Ground
- **Location / time:** Artisan and goldsmith streets; day
- **Source:** HWT-0100
- **Characters / institutions:** Kōvalaṉ, craft workers, market intermediaries
- **Form / lock:** SOURCE_DIRECT · LOCKED_SOURCE
- **Source action:** Kōvalaṉ enters specialised artisan streets carrying the valuable anklet.
- **Dramatic function:** Separate him from family, guild and local merchant protection at the moment of transaction.
- **Turn:** The object meant to restore status exposes his lack of local standing.
- **Performance focus:** Formal merchant courtesy, careful presentation and reliance on specialist knowledge.
- **Production requirements:** Goldsmith quarter, tools, furnaces, scales, security and anklet continuity.
- **Transition out:** The goldsmith recognises the object and an opportunity to redirect suspicion.
- **Interpretive limit:** Do not portray Kōvalaṉ as commercially naïve in every respect; his vulnerability is relational and institutional.

## SCN-MDU-007 — The goldsmith converts expertise into deception
- **Canto:** 16. Execution Ground
- **Location / time:** Goldsmith workspace and route toward authority; continuous
- **Source:** HWT-0101
- **Characters / institutions:** Goldsmith, Kōvalaṉ, palace-linked authority
- **Form / lock:** SOURCE_DIRECT · LOCKED_SOURCE
- **Source action:** The goldsmith falsely identifies Kōvalaṉ as the thief connected to the queen’s anklet.
- **Dramatic function:** Show how specialised credibility can manufacture a suspect.
- **Turn:** A private sale attempt becomes a criminal classification.
- **Performance focus:** The deception is controlled and administratively useful rather than melodramatic confession.
- **Production requirements:** Craft comparison, secure movement of information, palace-service interface.
- **Transition out:** The claim reaches coercive authority without material testing.
- **Interpretive limit:** Individual deceit must not erase the sovereign duty to investigate.

## SCN-MDU-008 — The order outruns inquiry
- **Canto:** 16. Execution Ground
- **Location / time:** Palace decision chain and city movement; day
- **Source:** HWT-0102
- **Characters / institutions:** King or royal authority, officers, goldsmith, Kōvalaṉ
- **Form / lock:** SOURCE_COMPOSITE · LOCKED_ADAPTATION
- **Source action:** A wrongful execution proceeds without adequate examination of the anklet or accusation.
- **Dramatic function:** Centre the catastrophe on institutional haste.
- **Turn:** The state treats an untested assertion as sufficient for irreversible force.
- **Performance focus:** Orders move through hierarchy faster than Kōvalaṉ’s words can enter it.
- **Production requirements:** Messenger and guard chain, palace threshold, custody blocking and compressed time.
- **Transition out:** Kōvalaṉ is taken beyond the market’s ordinary protections.
- **Interpretive limit:** Do not add a full modern trial that the text does not provide.

## SCN-MDU-009 — Kōvalaṉ is killed as a classified body
- **Canto:** 16. Execution Ground
- **Location / time:** Execution place; day
- **Source:** HWT-0102
- **Characters / institutions:** Kōvalaṉ, officers or executioners, absent royal decision
- **Form / lock:** SOURCE_DIRECT · LOCKED_SOURCE
- **Source action:** Kōvalaṉ is unlawfully killed before the evidence is properly examined.
- **Dramatic function:** Make correction impossible and transform the anklet into posthumous proof.
- **Turn:** The recovery plan ends in state violence.
- **Performance focus:** Confusion, failed access to procedure and abrupt bodily handling; no heroic foreknowledge.
- **Production requirements:** Execution-ground research, restrained violence, body and blood continuity.
- **Transition out:** The pastoral kuravai is interrupted by the city’s cry.
- **Interpretive limit:** Do not frame the death as deserved punishment for Kōvalaṉ’s earlier relationships.

## SCN-MDU-010 — The city cry breaks the dance
- **Canto:** 18. Garland of Sorrow
- **Location / time:** Mādhari’s settlement; kuravai ending
- **Source:** HWT-0114
- **Characters / institutions:** Kaṇṇaki, Mādhari, pastoral women, messenger or city voice
- **Form / lock:** MIXED_PERFORMANCE · LOCKED_SOURCE
- **Source action:** A public cry interrupts the completed kuravai and reports the accusation and killing.
- **Dramatic function:** Transfer narrative agency from the dead Kōvalaṉ to Kaṇṇaki.
- **Turn:** Communal repair collapses into specific irreversible news.
- **Performance focus:** Rhythm stops, bodies lose assigned roles and Kaṇṇaki receives language her body cannot immediately contain.
- **Production requirements:** Precise sound interruption, ensemble reaction and continuity from ritual to grief.
- **Transition out:** Begins `SCN-MDU-011`, Kaṇṇaki’s grief and demand for truth.
- **Interpretive limit:** Do not soften the news into private notification; its public transmission matters.
