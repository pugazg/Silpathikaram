# Madurai Scene Architecture — Part 3B

## Scope

Scenes `SCN-MDU-026`–`SCN-MDU-030` show the city’s institutional collapse, Madurāpati’s intervention, the widening causal frame, Kaṇṇaki’s departure and the transition from civic judgement into Cēra memory.

## SCN-MDU-026 — The city loses its protectors
- **Canto:** 22. Fire
- **Location / time:** Palace, streets and sacred thresholds; fire continuing
- **Source:** HWT-0137, HWT-0138, HWT-0139, HWT-0140, HWT-0141, HWT-0142
- **Characters / institutions:** Guardian deities, palace, households, citizens and protected groups
- **Form / lock:** SOURCE_COMPOSITE · LOCKED_ADAPTATION
- **Source action:** Protective deities withdraw, the old city burns, households and palace share the consequence, protected categories remain distinguished, and cries replace normal urban sound.
- **Dramatic function:** Show the collapse of a whole governing environment rather than a sequence of isolated fire images.
- **Turn:** The capital established as ordered in `SCN-MDU-001` can no longer perform protection, administration or ordinary domestic life.
- **Performance focus:** Guards, householders and ritual workers discover that familiar procedures no longer function.
- **Production requirements:** Multi-zone fire continuity, palace damage, household evacuation, sacred thresholds and layered lament sound.
- **Transition out:** Citizens confront fear and grief as the city’s own deity approaches Kaṇṇaki.
- **Interpretive limit:** Do not depict protected categories as untouched abstractions; survival may still include terror, displacement and loss.

## SCN-MDU-027 — Madurai witnesses its transformation
- **Canto:** 22. Fire; 23. Madurāpati
- **Location / time:** Burning city edge; later
- **Source:** HWT-0143, HWT-0144
- **Characters / institutions:** Citizens, Kaṇṇaki, Madurāpati
- **Form / lock:** MIXED_PERFORMANCE · DEVELOPMENT
- **Source action:** Citizens experience fear and grief; Madurāpati, the city deity, appears before Kaṇṇaki.
- **Dramatic function:** Shift from collective catastrophe to a confrontation between the civic accuser and the sacred identity of the city.
- **Turn:** Madurai itself gains a speaking representative only after human institutions have failed.
- **Performance focus:** The deity approaches with caution or humility appropriate to speaking after irreversible loss.
- **Production requirements:** City-deity design grounded in the source world, reduced fire intensity and citizen witness continuity.
- **Transition out:** Madurāpati explains the wider causal history.
- **Interpretive limit:** Do not portray the deity as arriving to defeat, silence or morally correct Kaṇṇaki.

## SCN-MDU-028 — Explanation follows proof
- **Canto:** 23. Madurāpati
- **Location / time:** Ruined or withdrawing Madurai; continuous
- **Source:** HWT-0145, HWT-0146
- **Characters / institutions:** Madurāpati, Kaṇṇaki, remembered king and causal past
- **Form / lock:** SOURCE_COMPOSITE · LOCKED_SOURCE
- **Source action:** Madurāpati explains a larger causal framework while the king’s immediate injustice remains central and Kaṇṇaki is affirmed within the category of pattini.
- **Dramatic function:** Hold karma, political failure and human responsibility together without letting any one erase the others.
- **Turn:** Kaṇṇaki receives context, not reversal or restitution.
- **Performance focus:** She listens from exhaustion and grief; explanation does not restore husband, city or court.
- **Production requirements:** Dialogue or mixed-verse strategy, memory fragments and restrained sacred sound.
- **Transition out:** The deity marks the duration and direction of Kaṇṇaki’s movement beyond the city.
- **Interpretive limit:** Karma must never be used to absolve the goldsmith, officers or king.

## SCN-MDU-029 — Fourteen days beyond the city
- **Canto:** 23. Madurāpati
- **Location / time:** Route away from Madurai; marked passage of days
- **Source:** HWT-0147, HWT-0148
- **Characters / institutions:** Kaṇṇaki, changing landscape, sacred transition
- **Form / lock:** SOURCE_COMPOSITE · DEVELOPMENT
- **Source action:** The narrative marks a fourteen-day transition and moves Kaṇṇaki beyond ordinary civic space toward the heavenly or sacred domain.
- **Dramatic function:** Let bodily departure, grief and sacred transformation occur over duration rather than through an instant polished icon.
- **Turn:** The public accuser leaves the city whose case she proved and becomes increasingly available through testimony rather than ordinary social presence.
- **Performance focus:** Reduced speech, altered pace, damaged body and fragments of remembered sound.
- **Production requirements:** Day-count continuity, westward or onward landscape research and gradual costume/body transition.
- **Transition out:** Hill witnesses become the next holders of her story.
- **Interpretive limit:** The exact visualisation of ascent or sacred transition remains an open adaptation decision.

## SCN-MDU-030 — Judgement becomes transferable memory
- **Canto:** Madurai closing arc and Vañci handoff
- **Location / time:** Formal transition from Madurai aftermath to Cēra hill testimony
- **Source:** HWT-0149, HWT-0150, HWT-0151, HWT-0152
- **Characters / institutions:** Kaṇṇaki as remembered presence, ruined Madurai, future witnesses
- **Form / lock:** CONTROLLED_BRIDGE · LOCKED_ADAPTATION
- **Source action:** The completed Madurai arc combines political failure, moral consequence and sacred transformation; production interpretation must retain selective fire, and the closing prepares Cēra memorialisation.
- **Dramatic function:** Close the justice-state arc and establish that Vañci begins from testimony about an event already morally complete.
- **Turn:** Kaṇṇaki’s direct civic action becomes a story that communities and rulers will receive, frame and transmit.
- **Performance focus:** The surviving anklet sound, witness memory or hill-landscape entry replaces explanatory recap.
- **Production requirements:** Ruined-city image, landscape bridge, testimony motif and continuity into `SCN-VAN-001`.
- **Transition out:** Begins the Vañci Scene Architecture with local hill recognition before royal action.
- **Interpretive limit:** Do not make the Cēra state the creator of Kaṇṇaki’s sacred truth.
