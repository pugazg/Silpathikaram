# Madurai Scene Architecture — Part 2A

## Scope

Scenes `SCN-MDU-011`–`SCN-MDU-015` transform Kaṇṇaki’s bodily collapse into a witnessed civic claim and introduce the queen’s warning dream before the court receives her.

## SCN-MDU-011 — Grief names royal error
- **Canto:** 18. Garland of Sorrow
- **Location / time:** Mādhari’s settlement; day
- **Source:** HWT-0115, HWT-0116
- **Characters / institutions:** Kaṇṇaki, pastoral women, city voice
- **Form / lock:** MIXED_PERFORMANCE · LOCKED_SOURCE
- **Source action:** Kaṇṇaki collapses, repeatedly identifies royal error and receives celestial confirmation that Kōvalaṉ was not a thief.
- **Dramatic function:** Move grief toward a claim about government without making the disembodied voice replace later proof.
- **Turn:** Her husband’s death becomes a public injustice rather than private bereavement alone.
- **Performance focus:** Broken breath, repetition and difficulty standing before speech regains direction.
- **Production requirements:** Ensemble grief blocking, controlled voice design and continuity from the interrupted kuravai.
- **Transition out:** Kaṇṇaki takes up the surviving anklet and moves toward the city.
- **Interpretive limit:** Celestial confirmation must not make the court test unnecessary.

## SCN-MDU-012 — The anklet becomes a public claim
- **Canto:** 19. City Circumambulation
- **Location / time:** Madurai streets; day
- **Source:** HWT-0117, HWT-0118
- **Characters / institutions:** Kaṇṇaki, citizens, surviving anklet
- **Form / lock:** SOURCE_COMPOSITE · LOCKED_SOURCE
- **Source action:** Kaṇṇaki carries the remaining anklet through Madurai while citizens recognise that the supposedly straight sceptre has bent.
- **Dramatic function:** Convert intimate property into visible evidence and civic judgement.
- **Turn:** The city begins interpreting its ruler through her suffering.
- **Performance focus:** She seeks witnesses rather than sympathy; citizens respond through recognition, distance and gathering.
- **Production requirements:** Street continuity, anklet handling, crowd escalation and sceptre imagery kept indirect.
- **Transition out:** Citizens guide her toward Kōvalaṉ’s body.
- **Interpretive limit:** Do not stage universal instant agreement or organised rebellion.

## SCN-MDU-013 — The crowd leads her to the body
- **Canto:** 19. City Circumambulation
- **Location / time:** Public street or execution aftermath; day
- **Source:** HWT-0119, HWT-0120
- **Characters / institutions:** Kaṇṇaki, citizens, Kōvalaṉ’s body
- **Form / lock:** SOURCE_DIRECT · LOCKED_SOURCE
- **Source action:** The city crowd guides Kaṇṇaki to the bloodied body, which she embraces and laments.
- **Dramatic function:** Make the public physically witness what royal procedure produced.
- **Turn:** Abstract accusation becomes an undeniable body in civic space.
- **Performance focus:** Recognition before touch, collapse into contact and the crowd’s inability to repair the loss.
- **Production requirements:** Body continuity, restrained blood effects, crowd sightlines and silence after movement.
- **Transition out:** Her lament becomes a set of questions addressed to the city’s moral order.
- **Interpretive limit:** Do not aestheticise the body into heroic martyr iconography.

## SCN-MDU-014 — Questions become a decision
- **Canto:** 19. City Circumambulation
- **Location / time:** Beside the body and route toward the palace; continuous
- **Source:** HWT-0121, HWT-0122
- **Characters / institutions:** Kaṇṇaki, citizens, women, elders, gods as invoked categories
- **Form / lock:** MIXED_PERFORMANCE · LOCKED_SOURCE
- **Source action:** Kaṇṇaki asks whether women, elders and gods remain in such a city and resolves not to rest before confronting the king.
- **Dramatic function:** Transform lament into chosen legal action.
- **Turn:** She leaves the body not because grief is complete, but because authority must answer.
- **Performance focus:** Questions expand outward; the final decision becomes quieter and more controlled.
- **Production requirements:** Route-to-palace geography, crowd handoff and anklet/body continuity.
- **Transition out:** The narrative cuts to the palace, where the queen’s dream has already disturbed royal confidence.
- **Interpretive limit:** Do not rewrite the questions as a modern political speech detached from their lament form.

## SCN-MDU-015 — The queen dreams the symbols of collapse
- **Canto:** 20. Court Case
- **Location / time:** Palace interior; before Kaṇṇaki’s arrival
- **Source:** HWT-0123
- **Characters / institutions:** Pāṇḍya queen, attendants, sceptre, parasol and gate bell
- **Form / lock:** MIXED_PERFORMANCE · DEVELOPMENT
- **Source action:** The queen dreams of falling royal emblems, a trembling bell, darkness and celestial disorder.
- **Dramatic function:** Let the palace register danger without understanding the specific injustice.
- **Turn:** Royal space becomes internally unsettled before the claimant reaches the gate.
- **Performance focus:** The queen attempts to report and interpret signs while court routine continues around her.
- **Production requirements:** Reusable royal objects, controlled dream grammar, bell sound and palace morning routine.
- **Transition out:** Her retinue moves into court as Kaṇṇaki’s gate demand interrupts the palace order.
- **Interpretive limit:** The dream must not show the anklet proof or make the queen knowingly complicit.
