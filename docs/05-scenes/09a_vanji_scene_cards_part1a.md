# Vañci Scene Architecture — Part 1A

## Scope

Scenes `SCN-VAN-001`–`SCN-VAN-005` begin with Kaṇṇaki’s encounter with hill women and establish local recognition, ritual practice, mountain ecology and Murukaṉ-associated sacred geography before any Cēra royal intervention.

## SCN-VAN-001 — Kaṇṇaki names the catastrophe
- **Canto:** 24. Hill Kuravai
- **Location / time:** Beneath the vēṅkai tree in the hill country; after Madurai
- **Source:** HWT-0153
- **Characters / institutions:** Kaṇṇaki, hill women
- **Form / lock:** SOURCE_DIRECT · LOCKED_SOURCE
- **Source action:** Kaṇṇaki identifies herself as the woman who lost her husband when Madurai and its kingship were destroyed.
- **Dramatic function:** Begin Vañci with her own compressed account rather than a royal interpretation.
- **Turn:** The direct civic actor becomes a stranger whose history must be received by a new community.
- **Performance focus:** Exhausted factual speech, damaged body and the hill women’s attempt to understand scale.
- **Production requirements:** Vēṅkai-tree setting, post-journey body continuity and restrained hill-community staging.
- **Transition out:** The women move from questioning to recognising a sacred obligation.
- **Interpretive limit:** Do not make Kaṇṇaki deliver a polished retrospective summary or claim royal sponsorship.

## SCN-VAN-002 — A small settlement receives a deity
- **Canto:** 24. Hill Kuravai
- **Location / time:** Hill settlement and ritual boundary; continuous
- **Source:** HWT-0154, HWT-0155
- **Characters / institutions:** Hill community, Kaṇṇaki, local ritual workers
- **Form / lock:** MIXED_PERFORMANCE · RESEARCH_HOLD
- **Source action:** The community is instructed to receive the one-breasted woman as a deity using drums, horn, bell, song, incense, flowers and protective boundaries.
- **Dramatic function:** Establish the first organised Pattini worship as local communal action.
- **Turn:** Recognition becomes material practice before court, monument or state decree.
- **Performance focus:** People gather implements, assign tasks and construct a protected ritual space.
- **Production requirements:** Small drums, horn, bell, incense, flowers, boundary-making and source-critical ritual consultation.
- **Transition out:** Ritual sound opens onto the water and stone world from which the community speaks.
- **Interpretive limit:** Do not retroactively reproduce the later Vañci shrine or assimilate the rite automatically to later theology.

## SCN-VAN-003 — Mountain water holds the community
- **Canto:** 24. Hill Kuravai
- **Location / time:** Waterfalls, pools and mineral-coloured rock; day
- **Source:** HWT-0156
- **Characters / institutions:** Hill women, water ecology, Kaṇṇaki as received presence
- **Form / lock:** MIXED_PERFORMANCE · RESEARCH_HOLD
- **Source action:** Waterfalls, pools, stone colour, flowers and mountain water structure the women’s ecological and performative world.
- **Dramatic function:** Make local worship inseparable from specific terrain rather than portable generic ritual.
- **Turn:** Kaṇṇaki’s memory is located within a living mountain ecology.
- **Performance focus:** Movement follows water depth, rock texture, gathering and communal play or song.
- **Production requirements:** Safe waterfall staging, mineral-colour research, wet costume continuity and ecological sound.
- **Transition out:** Water songs turn toward the spear and named Murukaṉ landscapes.
- **Interpretive limit:** Do not treat the mountain as untouched fantasy wilderness or decorative scenery.

## SCN-VAN-004 — The spear links distinct sacred landscapes
- **Canto:** 24. Hill Kuravai
- **Location / time:** Hill kuravai performance; continuing
- **Source:** HWT-0157
- **Characters / institutions:** Hill women, Murukaṉ-associated places, spear and remembered combat
- **Form / lock:** MIXED_PERFORMANCE · RESEARCH_HOLD
- **Source action:** The kuravai links Centil, Ceṅkōṭu, Veṇkuṉṟu and other Murukaṉ-associated landscapes through the spear and remembered divine combat.
- **Dramatic function:** Preserve a network of early Tamil sacred geography rather than collapse it into one temple image.
- **Turn:** Local song opens outward to a wider landscape tradition while remaining rooted in hill performance.
- **Performance focus:** Place names, spear gesture and collective memory carry meaning without explanatory theology.
- **Production requirements:** Spear iconography, movement research, vocal geography and restraint in divine visualisation.
- **Transition out:** The performance moves from remembered combat to debated healing and marriage practice.
- **Interpretive limit:** Do not automatically equate the source system with later standardised Murugan theology or iconography.

## SCN-VAN-005 — Ritual efficacy is debated inside the song
- **Canto:** 24. Hill Kuravai
- **Location / time:** Hill ritual gathering; continuing
- **Source:** HWT-0158
- **Characters / institutions:** Hill women, possession specialist, lovers and marriage community
- **Form / lock:** MIXED_PERFORMANCE · DEVELOPMENT
- **Source action:** Possession ritual and Murukaṉ worship are debated as responses to love-sickness and the desire for marriage.
- **Dramatic function:** Show a community interpreting ritual rather than presenting belief as uniform or unquestioned.
- **Turn:** Sacred performance becomes social argument about healing, desire and marriage.
- **Performance focus:** Counter-voices, teasing, concern and practical stakes remain audible within the kuravai.
- **Production requirements:** Ensemble vocal design, possession-practice consultation and clear differentiation of viewpoints.
- **Transition out:** The women redirect song toward the Pattini and the destruction of unjust Madurai.
- **Interpretive limit:** Do not diagnose the participants through modern medical categories or present possession as objective fraud or certainty.
