# Material Culture Bible — Part 2

## Scope

This part governs ritual sound and offerings, royal emblems, goldsmith tools and pastoral household objects.

## 7. Ritual sound and offering systems

### Road hunter community

- **Anchors:** HWT-0075–HWT-0080
- drums, horn, flute, bell, offerings, costume transformation, protective space and animal symbols.

### Hill Pattini recognition

- **Anchors:** HWT-0154–HWT-0159
- small drums, horn, bell, song, incense, flowers and settlement boundary.

### Installed Vañci shrine

- **Anchors:** HWT-0193–HWT-0194
- worked image, ornament, flower offering, protective boundary, sacrifice, festival and daily observance.

### Continuity rule

The three systems must remain visibly and acoustically distinct. The early hill rite cannot look or sound like the completed state shrine.

### Caution

Do not merge Korravai, Murukaṉ, Pattini and other source traditions into one generic ritual package.

## 8. Royal emblems and authority objects

### Pāṇḍya system

- sceptre;
- white parasol;
- gate bell;
- palace ceremonial objects;
- queen’s retinue objects.

**Anchors:** HWT-0123–HWT-0130

### Cēra system

- sword and parasol turned north;
- proclamation drum;
- written royal documents;
- military standards;
- victory and return emblems.

**Anchors:** HWT-0169–HWT-0179, HWT-0190

### Design rule

Royal objects must work in routine administration before becoming spectacle, dream symbols, campaign emblems or signs of collapse.

### Caution

Do not reuse one generic royal-prop package for Pāṇḍya and Cēra authority.

## 9. Goldsmith tools and evidence handling

### Source control

- **Anchors:** HWT-0100–HWT-0102
- **Status:** `HISTORICAL_RESEARCH`

### Research set

- furnace and fuel;
- crucibles;
- hammers, punches and tongs;
- scales and weights;
- gem and pearl handling;
- secure storage;
- palace-service transport.

### Evidence-handling requirements

- establish ordinary inspection behaviour before deception;
- distinguish the goldsmith’s own tools from royal-custody handling;
- preserve anklet wrapping, orientation and condition;
- track who sees and touches the object;
- maintain clear separation between claim and tested fact.

### Caution

The goldsmith’s authority comes from credible specialised work. His tools cannot look theatrical, improvised or visibly criminal.

## 10. Pastoral household objects

### Source control

- **Anchors:** HWT-0098–HWT-0099, HWT-0108–HWT-0114

### Requirements

- milk, curd and butter vessels;
- churning and heating equipment;
- cattle bells and ropes;
- feed and water systems;
- cooking and guest-care vessels;
- sleeping and washing arrangements;
- kuravai musical and role-assignment objects.

### Normal-process rule

The audience must understand the ordinary function of milk, butter, calves, bells and vessels before the omens disrupt them.

### Caution

- Do not reduce Mādhari’s household to rustic decoration.
- Do not stage pastoral women as interchangeable background performers.
- Do not use generic metal vessels where source-era material research is unresolved.

## Cross-system differentiation table

| System | Primary material logic | Sound logic | Authority form |
|---|---|---|---|
| Hunter ritual | locally gathered offerings, costume transformation and protective enclosure | drum, horn, flute and bell | communal ritual obligation |
| Hill Pattini recognition | flowers, incense, boundary and mountain materials | small drum, horn, bell and song | local witness and reception |
| Pāṇḍya court | sceptre, parasol, bell and controlled object access | ceremonial order and gate transmission | sovereign administration |
| Cēra mobilisation | sword, parasol, drum, documents and standards | proclamation, praise and command | consultative state expedition |
| Pastoral refuge | dairy, cattle, food and guest-care objects | labour rhythms, bells and kuravai | household protection |
