# Costume and Character Material Progression

## Purpose

This layer governs costume change as character action. Clothing, ornament, repair, removal and condition must follow source-linked emotional and social movement.

## Kaṇṇaki

### State 1 — Merchant household

- Source: HWT-0030-HWT-0035
- Material logic: quality, household belonging, controlled prosperity.
- Avoid: royal excess.

### State 2 — Separation

- Source: HWT-0043
- Material change: removal and reduction of ornament.
- Meaning: disciplined subtraction, not sudden poverty.

### State 3 — Traveller

- Source: HWT-0059-HWT-0076
- Material change: dust, repair, reduced possessions, bodily wear.

### State 4 — Mādhari refuge

- Source: HWT-0098-HWT-0099
- Material change: temporary restoration through care.

### State 5 — Court claimant

- Source: HWT-0125-HWT-0129
- Material change: surviving anklet becomes visible evidence.

### State 6 — Sacred memory

- Source: HWT-0153-HWT-0196
- Caution: do not apply completed goddess iconography before community recognition.

## Kōvalaṉ

### Material arc

- Merchant confidence → artistic luxury → shame and reduction → travel wear → trader without protection.

Rules:

- loss must be visible before Madurai;
- his clothing must not erase his merchant identity completely;
- execution should occur after social vulnerability becomes visible.

## Mādhavi

### Material arc

- Training discipline → debut artist → publicly honoured performer → intimate partner → letter writer → renunciant.

Rules:

- costume is professional equipment, not decoration;
- performance objects require maintenance history;
- renunciation is transformation, not purification.

## Kauṇṭi

### Material arc

- Ascetic practicality → travel durability → protective authority.

Rules:

- every object must have practical use;
- avoid generic holy-person imagery.

## World costume systems

### Pukār

Status through work, exchange, performance and household role.

### Road

Condition change through terrain, water, heat and distance.

### Madurai

Authority through access, court procedure and civic position.

### Vañci

Memory through ritual participation, specialist labour and institution.

## Locked principles

1. Costume follows character history.
2. Ornament cannot replace social function.
3. Material decline and recovery require continuity.
4. Sacred appearance emerges through community and ritual process.
5. All major costume decisions require source IDs and scene IDs.
