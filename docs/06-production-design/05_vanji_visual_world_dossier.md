# Vañci Visual World Dossier

## Design thesis

Vañci is the world in which direct experience becomes shared memory, royal policy, expedition, monument and recurring worship. The design must preserve the order of authority:

**Kaṇṇaki speaks → hill community recognises → Kuravar witness → Sāttanār transmits → court decides → state acts → specialists build → wider communities participate**.

Royal power institutionalises memory; it does not create Kaṇṇaki’s truth.

## Source control

- **Primary anchors:** HWT-0153–HWT-0197
- **Scene architecture:** `SCN-VAN-001`–`SCN-VAN-030`
- **Status:** mixed `LOCKED_SOURCE`, `HISTORICAL_RESEARCH`, `DEVELOPMENT` and `CAUTION`

## 1. Hill encounter and local worship

- **Primary anchors:** HWT-0153–HWT-0159
- **LOCKED_SOURCE:** Kaṇṇaki identifies herself beneath the vēṅkai tree; hill women receive the one-breasted woman through drums, horn, bell, song, incense, flowers and ritual boundaries.
- **Visual interpretation:** The first Pattini space is small, locally assembled and integrated with waterfall, stone, flowers and mountain movement.
- **Production implication:** Ritual implements must look gathered from an existing community rather than issued by a later shrine institution.
- **CAUTION:** Do not back-project the completed Vañci temple into this first recognition.

## 2. Mountain ecology and Murukaṉ-linked geography

- **Primary anchors:** HWT-0156–HWT-0158
- **LOCKED_SOURCE:** Waterfalls, mineral-coloured rock, named landscapes, spear memory, possession practice, healing and marriage debate shape the kuravai world.
- **Visual interpretation:** Sacred meaning travels through place names, water, spear gesture, song and social argument.
- **CAUTION:** Do not standardise the source into later temple iconography or erase disagreement within the community.

## 3. Royal hill excursion

- **Primary anchors:** HWT-0160–HWT-0164
- **LOCKED_SOURCE:** Senguttuvan and Iḷaṅkō Vēṇmāḷ enter mountain country with retinue amid hill songs, labour, elephant keepers, hunters, tribute and ecological knowledge.
- **Visual interpretation:** The court moves through an inhabited landscape and must adapt to terrain rather than visually own it.
- **Production implication:** Procession width, pack animals, tribute handling and local audience geometry must respect difficult ground.
- **CAUTION:** Avoid discovery-of-empty-land imagery.

## 4. Tribute and witness

- **Primary anchors:** HWT-0162–HWT-0165
- **LOCKED_SOURCE:** Ivory, eaglewood, honey, sandal, pigments, spices, produce, animals and birds enter court alongside Kuravar eyewitness testimony and Sāttanār’s historical account.
- **Visual interpretation:** Material tribute and verbal testimony are different knowledge forms and must not be staged as equivalent spectacle.
- **Production implication:** Goods require containers, accounting, animal-welfare alternatives and visible labour; witnesses require listening space and narrative distinction.
- **CAUTION:** Do not reduce hill communities to suppliers of exotic objects.

## 5. Vañci court and memorial debate

- **Primary anchors:** HWT-0166–HWT-0170
- **LOCKED_SOURCE:** Royal accountability, the queen’s Pattini proposal, stone-source debate, northern vow, proclamation and intelligence systems precede mobilisation.
- **Visual interpretation:** Court design should support consultation among ruler, queen, ministers, specialists and messengers rather than present solitary royal command.
- **Production implication:** Include map or oral-geography space, proclamation drums, scribal handling and formal debate positions.
- **CAUTION:** Do not make the expedition an impulsive adventure detached from memorial policy.

## 6. Mobilisation and administration

- **Primary anchors:** HWT-0171–HWT-0179
- **LOCKED_SOURCE:** Auspicious timing, sword and parasol orientation, councils, accounting, army provisioning, sacred departure honours, poets, heralds, elephants, cavalry, Nilagiri camp, ascetic instruction, alliance and written administration form the campaign system.
- **Visual interpretation:** War begins as logistics, food, records, animals, specialists and public performance before it becomes battle.
- **Production implication:** Build provisioning lines, grain and cooking systems, written documents, command tents, animal care, camp sanitation and guarded storage.
- **CAUTION:** Do not compress mobilisation into a decorative marching montage.

## 7. Northern battle and stone acquisition

- **Primary anchors:** HWT-0180–HWT-0183
- **LOCKED_SOURCE:** Named coalition, banners, drums, horns, infantry, chariots, elephants, horses, dust, wounds, capture and the stone carried on defeated royal heads belong to the victory narrative.
- **Visual interpretation:** Organised mass violence must remain costly and politically charged.
- **Production implication:** Distinguish units by command, movement and equipment; track veteran wounds into the return sequence.
- **CAUTION:** Do not aestheticise conquest as uncomplicated sacred triumph.

## 8. Ganges consecration complex

- **Primary anchors:** HWT-0184–HWT-0186
- **LOCKED_SOURCE:** Specialists consecrate the stone at the Ganges; temple, hall, arena, pavilion, royal quarters, gardens, ponds and performance areas form a temporary ceremonial complex.
- **Visual interpretation:** The site is an expedition-built ritual and political environment rather than a timeless finished temple.
- **Production implication:** Show assembly, labour, water access, temporary structures, security and ceremonial circulation.
- **CAUTION:** Avoid copying later monumental North Indian temple architecture without evidence.

## 9. Māṭalan and narrative reconnection

- **Primary anchor:** HWT-0187
- **LOCKED_SOURCE:** Māṭalan reconnects the northern achievement to Mādhavi, Kōvalaṉ, Kaṇṇaki, Mādhari, Kauṇṭi and bereaved families.
- **Visual interpretation:** Victory space is interrupted by the human cost accumulated across Pukār, Road and Madurai.
- **Production implication:** Use remembered objects, faces, songs or locations with clear source status rather than celebratory recap.
- **CAUTION:** The northern campaign must not replace the emotional centre of the epic.

## 10. Return to Vañci

- **Primary anchors:** HWT-0188–HWT-0192
- **LOCKED_SOURCE:** Released rulers, regional songs, triumphal entry, visible veteran wounds, prisoner release and civic remission precede completion of the Pattini institution.
- **Visual interpretation:** The return combines celebration, bodily damage and public policy.
- **Production implication:** Veteran makeup and equipment continuity must match battle records; different ecological regions retain distinct musical reception forms.
- **CAUTION:** Do not merge hill, agrarian, pastoral and coastal songs into one generic victory chorus.

## 11. Shrine construction and recurring worship

- **Primary anchors:** HWT-0193–HWT-0194
- **LOCKED_SOURCE:** Ritual experts, teachers, calendrical specialists and craftspeople collaborate; the installed image receives ornament, flower offerings, protective boundaries, festival and daily observance.
- **Visual interpretation:** The shrine emerges through visible design decisions, skilled labour and negotiated ritual order.
- **Production implication:** Show stone dressing, measurements, image installation, enclosure, offering storage, procession route and daily maintenance.
- **CAUTION:** Do not present the shrine as appearing complete through royal command alone.

## 12. Memory community and transregional participation

- **Primary anchors:** HWT-0195–HWT-0196
- **LOCKED_SOURCE:** Dēvanti, guardian woman, female companion and Aiyai carry personal memory; rulers from other regions seek participation and receive a grant.
- **Visual interpretation:** The shrine gathers unequal memories—bereaved women, witnesses, rulers and ritual specialists do not enter with the same authority.
- **Production implication:** Spatially distinguish intimate lament, public audience, diplomatic request and ritual participation.
- **CAUTION:** Do not let royal internationalisation silence the women who carry the lived memory.

## 13. Ethical conclusion

- **Primary anchor:** HWT-0197
- **LOCKED_SOURCE:** The epic closes with injunctions concerning truth, killing, theft, desire, association, charity, restraint, gratitude and fellowship.
- **Visual interpretation:** Final images should return ethical responsibility to ordinary conduct rather than end only on monument or king.
- **Production implication:** Consider recurring actions—food sharing, truthful speech, restraint, care for living beings and maintenance of communal spaces—without converting the close into a modern sermon montage.

## 14. Material and colour progression

### Hill recognition

- water-dark stone, flowers, smoke, fibre, drums and local metal sound.

### Court and expedition

- documents, parasol, sword, leather, timber, grain, pack systems and military dust.

### Consecration

- unfinished stone, river water, temporary ceremonial fabrics and specialist tools.

### Installed shrine

- worked stone, recurring flowers, boundary markers, maintained lamps and objects bearing accumulated handling.

These are **DEVELOPMENT** directions, not fixed source palettes.

## 15. Required research queue

1. Cēra-period hill routes and possible Vañci location debates.
2. Hill tribute goods, botanical identification and extraction systems.
3. Early Tamil royal councils, scribal practice and proclamation.
4. Elephant, cavalry, chariot and camp logistics.
5. Ganges-crossing fleet and alliance systems.
6. Stone extraction, transport, carving and installation.
7. Temporary ceremonial architecture.
8. Early Pattini shrine practices without later-period collapse.
9. Regional music and song distinctions in the return sequence.

## 16. Visual continuity locks

- Local recognition always precedes royal institutionalisation.
- The Kuravar remain eyewitnesses, not decorative tribute bearers.
- The queen participates in policy formation.
- War logistics and veteran wounds remain visible.
- Māṭalan restores the earlier human stories inside the victory arc.
- Shrine construction requires specialists and labour.
- Women’s memory remains present after the institution becomes transregional.
- The final emphasis returns to ethical life, not royal possession.
