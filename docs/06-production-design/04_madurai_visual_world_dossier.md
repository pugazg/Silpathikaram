# Madurai Visual World Dossier

## Design thesis

Madurai is an organised capital whose visible competence makes its justice failure more devastating. Fortification, differentiated markets, artisan authority, pastoral refuge, palace procedure and civic witness must all appear functional before the city’s institutions collapse.

The design must support a precise causal movement:

**commercial vulnerability → false classification → sovereign haste → material proof → selective catastrophe**.

## Source control

- **Primary anchors:** HWT-0090–HWT-0152
- **Scene architecture:** `SCN-MDU-001`–`SCN-MDU-030`
- **Status:** mixed `LOCKED_SOURCE`, `HISTORICAL_RESEARCH`, `DEVELOPMENT` and `CAUTION`

## 1. Fortified capital and urban approach

- **Primary anchors:** HWT-0090–HWT-0097
- **LOCKED_SOURCE:** Gardens, cultivated edges, Vaigai activity, moat, walls, guarded gates, sacred institutions, markets, performance labour and river use establish an ordered city.
- **Visual interpretation:** Urban authority appears through maintained thresholds, specialised streets and coordinated movement rather than monumental emptiness.
- **Production implication:** Riverfront, gate, market and palace axes must connect geographically.
- **CAUTION:** Do not signal corruption through universal dirt, cruelty or sinister architecture from the first scene.

## 2. Outer refuge and pastoral household

- **Primary anchors:** HWT-0098–HWT-0099, HWT-0108–HWT-0114
- **LOCKED_SOURCE:** Kauṇṭi entrusts Kaṇṇaki to Mādhari; food, water, cattle, dairy work, omens and kuravai belong to a pastoral household system outside central authority.
- **Visual interpretation:** The refuge should contrast with the capital through work scale and social intimacy, not through poverty caricature.
- **Production implication:** Cattle space, dairy vessels, cooking, sleeping arrangements and ritual-performance area must form one credible settlement.
- **CAUTION:** Do not merge Mādhari’s household with the earlier outer religious settlement.

## 3. Artisan and goldsmith quarter

- **Primary anchors:** HWT-0100–HWT-0102
- **LOCKED_SOURCE:** Kōvalaṉ enters specialised craft streets carrying the anklet; the goldsmith’s expertise gives his false accusation administrative credibility.
- **Visual interpretation:** Tools, furnaces, scales, secure workspaces and palace-linked service distinguish authority based on craft knowledge.
- **Production implication:** The goldsmith’s workspace must permit comparison, concealment, message transmission and guarded custody.
- **CAUTION:** Do not portray all artisans as corrupt or turn the goldsmith into a visually obvious villain.

## 4. Anklet evidence system

- **Primary anchors:** HWT-0100–HWT-0102, HWT-0124–HWT-0130
- **LOCKED_SOURCE:** Kaṇṇaki’s anklet contains gems; the queen’s contains pearls; breaking the object supplies decisive proof.
- **Production implication:** Create matched external forms with unmistakably different internal contents, hero props, breakaway copies and continuity records.
- **Visual interpretation:** The court should initially read ornament and social status; the proof forces attention to construction and contents.
- **CAUTION:** The evidence must not appear magical. Its force is material and procedural.

## 5. Palace gate and court geometry

- **Primary anchors:** HWT-0123–HWT-0130
- **LOCKED_SOURCE:** Queen’s warning dream, ceremonial retinue, gatekeeper transmission, royal court, claimant’s identity statement, material test and royal collapse form a continuous institutional sequence.
- **Visual interpretation:** The gate controls access; the court controls sightlines and speech; Kaṇṇaki disrupts both by carrying testable evidence.
- **Production implication:** Design clear routes from gate to ruler, positions for queen and officials, object presentation space and public witness.
- **CAUTION:** Do not import a modern courtroom bench, advocate system or prolonged formal trial.

## 6. Civic witness

- **Primary anchors:** HWT-0115–HWT-0122
- **LOCKED_SOURCE:** Grief becomes public claim through streets, citizens, the surviving anklet and Kōvalaṉ’s body.
- **Visual interpretation:** Crowds gather unevenly; some guide, some observe, some withdraw. The city is not one unanimous mass.
- **Production implication:** Street routes must connect refuge, body, civic gathering and palace.
- **CAUTION:** Avoid modern protest iconography or instantaneous organised rebellion.

## 7. Royal emblems

- **Primary anchors:** HWT-0123, HWT-0129–HWT-0130
- **LOCKED_SOURCE:** Sceptre, parasol, gate bell and court order carry political meaning and participate in dream and collapse.
- **Visual interpretation:** Emblems should be working objects used in routine before becoming signs of failure.
- **Production implication:** Maintain exact object continuity from ordinary court scenes through dream, proof and collapse.

## 8. Fire and selective protection

- **Primary anchors:** HWT-0131–HWT-0144
- **LOCKED_SOURCE:** Kaṇṇaki’s vow, three circuits, embodied accusation, fire deity, protected categories, selective ignition, withdrawing guardians and city-wide collapse govern the destruction.
- **Production implication:** Create a zone map distinguishing ignition, protected routes, palace, households, sacred thresholds and evacuation movement.
- **Visual interpretation:** Fire acts according to the epic’s stated moral categories while remaining terrifying and materially destructive.
- **CAUTION:** Do not depict an indiscriminate natural disaster. Do not make selectivity clean, painless or simplistic.

## 9. Madurāpati and aftermath

- **Primary anchors:** HWT-0143–HWT-0152
- **LOCKED_SOURCE:** Madurāpati approaches after institutional failure, supplies a wider causal explanation and marks Kaṇṇaki’s movement beyond the city.
- **Visual interpretation:** The city deity should carry Madurai’s specific civic identity, not appear as a generic fantasy goddess.
- **CAUTION:** Do not make Madurāpati defeat, silence or absolve Kaṇṇaki; karma must not erase immediate human and royal responsibility.

## 10. Material and colour progression

### Before failure

- maintained gates and plaster;
- differentiated commodity colour;
- polished court objects;
- measured artisan light;
- water and green cultivated edges.

### After wrongful death

- the same routes become obstructed by bodies, crowds and broken procedure;
- anklet colour and sound become dominant evidence motifs;
- royal symmetry loses coherence.

### During fire

- zone-specific smoke, ember and structural damage;
- protected spaces remain legible;
- sound shifts from administration and commerce to alarm, lament and withdrawal.

These are **DEVELOPMENT** directions rather than fixed source colour claims.

## 12. Visual continuity locks

- Madurai begins as credible order.
- Kōvalaṉ remains an outsider to local networks.
- The anklet is continuously traceable from household wealth to courtroom proof.
- The queen is witness and participant in collapse, not Kaṇṇaki’s rival.
- Fire follows explicit protected-category logic.
- The ruined city remains humanly inhabited and mourned.
