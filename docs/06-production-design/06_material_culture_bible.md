# Material Culture Bible

## Purpose

This file governs recurring objects across art direction, props, costume, performance, sound, VFX and continuity.

Objects are not decorative symbols added after the story. They carry wealth, labour, evidence, memory, authority and ritual action.

## Status key

- `LOCKED_SOURCE` — required by the Tamil source or concordance.
- `HISTORICAL_RESEARCH` — form requires external evidence.
- `DEVELOPMENT` — production interpretation.
- `CAUTION` — prohibited simplification or anachronism.

## 1. The anklet system

### Narrative functions

1. secured merchant-house wealth;
2. Kaṇṇaki’s contribution to economic recovery;
3. commodity entering Madurai’s artisan system;
4. falsely classified royal property;
5. posthumous evidence;
6. recurring sound and memory object.

### Source control

- **Anchors:** HWT-0058, HWT-0100–HWT-0102, HWT-0117–HWT-0118, HWT-0124–HWT-0130
- **Scenes:** `SCN-PUK-022`, `SCN-MDU-006`–`SCN-MDU-020`
- **Status:** `LOCKED_SOURCE`

### Prop requirements

- matched external silhouette for Kaṇṇaki’s and the queen’s anklets;
- clearly different internal contents: gems versus pearls;
- wearable hero pair for Pukār;
- travel-safe surviving anklet;
- goldsmith inspection version;
- court hero version;
- breakaway versions with controlled gem release;
- fragments and continuity trays.

### Sound

The anklet requires distinct states:

- paired domestic sound;
- wrapped or stored silence;
- handled transaction sound;
- isolated surviving-anklet sound;
- courtroom impact and gem scatter;
- later memory motif.

### Caution

- Do not make the object magical.
- Do not obscure the gem/pearl difference with excessive ornament.
- Do not replace material proof with confession or prophecy.

## 2. Yāḻ and musical instruments

### Source control

- **Anchors:** HWT-0036–HWT-0048 and performance-related Road/Vañci anchors
- **Status:** `LOCKED_SOURCE` for presence and dramatic use; `HISTORICAL_RESEARCH` for exact construction.

### Requirements

- instrument family research rather than one generic harp;
- tuning, string replacement, carrying and storage systems;
- performer posture and hand technique;
- separate rehearsal, stage and intimate-beach acoustic strategies;
- visible musician labour and hierarchy.

### Caution

- Do not use an unmodified modern harp or veena as a visual substitute.
- Do not treat musical instruments as silent costume accessories.

## 3. Flower-letter system

### Source control

- **Anchors:** HWT-0052–HWT-0053, HWT-0083–HWT-0085
- **Scenes:** `SCN-PUK-019`, `SCN-ROAD-015`–`SCN-ROAD-016`
- **Status:** `LOCKED_SOURCE`

### Requirements

- flower and plant identification research;
- scent logic;
- writing surface and inscription method;
- seal or identifying mark;
- transport protection;
- ageing between Pukār and the Road;
- exact handoff continuity.

### Caution

The letter corrects Kōvalaṉ’s interpretation of Mādhavi. It must not appear as a generic modern paper note.

## 5. Performance costumes and stage objects

### Source control

- **Anchors:** HWT-0036–HWT-0041, HWT-0023–HWT-0024

## 6. Travel equipment

### Source control

- **Anchors:** HWT-0061–HWT-0089
- **Status:** `HISTORICAL_RESEARCH` governed by source actions.

### Requirements

- footwear or barefoot continuity based on character and terrain;
- water containers;
- food wrapping;
- bedding and temporary shelter materials;
- load-carrying systems;
- washing and foot-care implements;
- rain, river and heat protection;
- letter and anklet security.

### Caution

Loads must reduce and wear over time. Characters cannot carry modern expedition packs or unlimited supplies.

## 7. Ritual sound and offering systems

### Road hunter community

- **Anchors:** HWT-0075–HWT-0080
- drums, horn, flute, bell, offerings, costume transformation, protective space and animal symbols.

### Hill Pattini recognition

- **Anchors:** HWT-0154–HWT-0159
- small drums, horn, bell, song, incense, flowers and settlement boundary.

### Installed Vañci shrine

- **Anchors:** HWT-0193–HWT-0194
- worked image, ornament, flower offering, protective boundary, sacrifice, festival and daily observance.

### Continuity rule

The three systems must remain visibly distinct. The early hill rite cannot look like the completed state shrine.

### Caution

Do not merge Korravai, Murukaṉ, Pattini and other source traditions into one generic ritual package.

## 11. Fire-sequence objects

- **Anchors:** HWT-0131–HWT-0144

### Object life cycle

1. stone-source debate;
2. Himalayan extraction;
3. transport and captive-king victory display;
4. Ganges consecration;
5. southward return;
6. specialist carving and shrine construction;
7. installation;
8. recurring offerings and maintenance.

### Source control

- **Anchors:** HWT-0168–HWT-0169, HWT-0182–HWT-0185, HWT-0193–HWT-0194
- **Status:** `LOCKED_SOURCE` for sequence; `HISTORICAL_RESEARCH` for technology and form.

### Caution

The image and shrine cannot appear complete through royal intention alone. Labour, tools, measurement, ritual specialists and time must remain visible.

## 14. Object continuity register

Every recurring hero object should receive:

- stable prop ID;
- source IDs;
- first and last scene IDs;
- ownership history;
- condition history;
- duplicate count;
- stunt/VFX versions;
- storage and handling restrictions;
- sound identity;
- unresolved historical questions.

Priority objects:

1. Kaṇṇaki anklet pair and surviving anklet;
2. queen’s anklet reference set;
3. Mādhavi’s yāḻ;
4. flower-letter;
5. Pāṇḍya sceptre, parasol and gate bell;
6. Cēra sword, parasol and proclamation drum;
7. Pattini stone stages and installed image;
8. ritual drums, horns and bells by community;
9. Kōvalaṉ and Kaṇṇaki travel loads;
10. Mādhari household dairy objects.

## 15. Department sign-off rule

No hero object is production-locked until the following fields are agreed:

- source fidelity;
- historical reconstruction status;
- performer usability;
- scene continuity;
- sound requirement;
- safety requirement;
- cultural and religious review;
- documented invention.
