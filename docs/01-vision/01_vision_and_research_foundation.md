# Silapathikaram Cinematic Research Bible

## Phase 1 — Vision and Research Foundation

Version: 0.1

> **Project goal:** Develop a source-traceable Tamil cinematic adaptation of *Cilappatikāram* that remains faithful to its literary architecture, moral force, performance traditions and plural early-Tamil world while functioning as serious cinema for a global audience.

---

## 1. Guiding principles

### Respect the epic

- Preserve the causal spine joining Pukār, Madurai and Vañji.
- Preserve Kaṇṇaki’s movement from household life through bereavement, public accusation, justice and transfiguration.
- Preserve Kōvalaṉ and Mādhavi as morally complex people rather than plot devices.
- Preserve the epic’s songs, dances, landscapes and public institutions as dramatic action, not decorative interludes.
- Preserve the anklet as legal evidence, intimate object, sound motif and title-bearing symbol.

### Cinema first, without source amnesia

The film must work through image, performance, rhythm, silence, sound and consequence. It must not become either an illustrated translation or a generic spectacle that borrows names while discarding the epic’s structure.

### Historical responsibility

Production design should be tested against early Tamil literature, archaeology, material culture, trade history and specialist scholarship. Later imperial Chola, medieval temple and modern classical-dance conventions must not be projected backward automatically.

### Ethical responsibility

- Do not romanticise abandonment, coercion, summary execution or collective destruction.
- Do not treat chastity as ownership of a woman’s body by a husband or society.
- Do not convert karma into victim-blaming or a substitute for institutional accountability.
- Separate literary age statements from casting: use adult performers for all mature relationship, coercion and violence material.
- Distinguish Kaṇṇaki’s moral authority from a superhero model of invulnerability.

---

## 2. Core adaptation principles

- Treat the three kāṇṭams as one causal design, not three loosely connected films.
- Make Pukār’s prosperity depend visibly on labour, trade, performance, household wealth and the sea.
- Let the Kōvalaṉ–Mādhavi relationship contain artistic intimacy, economic consequence and incompatible obligations without reducing Mādhavi to a seductress.
- Let Kaṇṇaki’s silence accumulate meaning before her public speech in Madurai.
- Stage the Pāṇḍya failure as a collapse of investigation and royal procedure, not merely one villain’s cruelty.
- Present the burning of Madurai as moral, civic and human catastrophe; spectacle must not erase suffering.
- Treat the hill communities’ recognition of Kaṇṇaki as an active cultural and religious response.
- Treat Cēraṉ Ceṅkuṭṭuvaṉ’s memorial project as politics, memory-making, ritual and interstate prestige—not a simple epilogue.
- Keep supernatural events sacred and consequential without allowing visual effects to replace dramatic meaning.
- Preserve religious plurality without flattening all traditions into a single modern category.

---

## 3. Evidence labels

Every research claim and adaptation choice should use one or more labels:

- **[TEXT]** — directly supported by *Cilappatikāram*.
- **[CROSS]** — supported by another early Tamil literary source after verification.
- **[HISTORY]** — supported by archaeology, inscriptions, numismatics or responsible historical scholarship.
- **[INTERPRETATION]** — a controlled cinematic reconstruction where evidence is incomplete.
- **[CAUTION]** — a later assumption, disputed identification or unsafe simplification that must not be presented as fact.

The labels identify the kind of support; they do not guarantee certainty. Each record also carries a verification status.

## 4. Canonical source hierarchy

### Primary project text

1. Tamil *Cilappatikāram* editions, to be edition-controlled.
2. The close English translations already committed in `cilappatikaram/`.
3. The translated critical study *Silambō Silambu!* in `criticism/`, used as scholarship rather than as a substitute for the epic.

### Cross-textual research

- *Manimekalai*
- *Pattinappālai*
- *Maturaikkāñci*
- *Patiṟṟuppattu*
- *Akanāṉūṟu*
- *Puṟanāṉūṟu*
- other relevant early Tamil texts after source and dating review

### Historical research

- archaeology of Kāvirippūmpattinam/Poompuhar and early historic Tamil urbanism;
- maritime trade, craft production, coins and exchange networks;
- Madurai’s political, ritual and material history;
- Chera geography, routes, memorial practice and interstate politics;
- music, theatre, dance, textiles, jewellery, food, law and religious plurality.

---

## 5. Dramatic centre

The adaptation is not simply the story of a wronged wife who destroys a city.

It is the story of how intimate choices, artistic life, commerce, migration, false testimony and failed government converge upon one object—the anklet—and force a private woman to become a public accuser whose truth outlives the institutions that failed her.

### Primary themes

- love, loyalty and incompatible obligations;
- wealth, labour and loss;
- art, reputation and autonomy;
- evidence, authority and justice;
- grief becoming speech;
- righteous anger and collective consequence;
- memory, deification and political appropriation;
- fate, causation and human responsibility.

## 7. World-building priorities

1. Pukār as maritime city, performance economy and household world.
2. The road as ecological, social and spiritual transition.
3. Madurai as market, court, neighbourhood and system of government.
4. The burning city as inhabited space, not an abstract backdrop.
5. Hill-country recognition of Kaṇṇaki.
6. Vañji and the Chera memorial project.
7. Material continuity of the anklets, clothing, wealth, travel and bodily exhaustion.

---

## 8. Research standards

- Cite the committed translation by book and canto; add line groups where available.
- Verify major dialogue and publicity claims against Tamil before locking them.
- Never use a later monument as automatic evidence for an earlier structure.
- Distinguish textual geography, historical geography and cinematic geography.
- Record uncertainty rather than hiding it.
- Make labour visible behind trade, luxury, performance, royal power and ritual.
- Require specialist review before finalising costume, music, dance, law, religion, language register, archaeology and VFX representations.

---

## 9. Immediate research programme

1. Complete canto-level evidence extraction from all three books.
2. Build full character bibles for the principal and institutional characters.
3. Create separate dossiers for Pukār, Madurai, hill country and Vañji.
4. Catalogue objects, foods, plants, animals, instruments, textiles and ornaments.
5. Map every legal and administrative action in the Madurai sequence.
6. Build a music-and-performance concordance.
7. Establish a scene register tied to evidence and adaptation decisions.
8. Commission Tamil, archaeology, theatre, dance, music, legal-history, religious-history and trauma-informed reviews.

---

## 10. Success criteria

The eventual screenplay should be defensible as:

- a faithful literary adaptation;
- a historically disciplined reconstruction wherever evidence permits;
- an explicit interpretation wherever evidence is incomplete;
- a humane treatment of coercion, grief and mass consequence;
- and a compelling cinematic work whose images remain accountable to its sources.
