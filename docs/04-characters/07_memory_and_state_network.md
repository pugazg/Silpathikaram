# Memory and State Network — Sāttanār, Iḷaṅkō Vēṇmāḷ, Māṭalan and Senguttuvan

## Purpose

Vañci is the politics of memory. These characters do not simply repeat Kaṇṇaki’s story. They select, transmit, interpret, advise and institutionalise it. The adaptation must show the difference between eyewitness testimony, narrative transmission, ethical counsel, royal policy and sacred installation.

## Sāttanār — narrative transmitter to the Cēra court

### Textual function

- **[TEXT]** After the Kuravar report the hill-country encounter, Sāttanār tells Senguttuvan the anklet case, Kaṇṇaki’s court action, the destruction of Madurai and the deaths of the Pāṇḍya king and queen.
- **[TEXT]** His narration converts dispersed events into a coherent political account for a ruler who did not witness them.

**Evidence:** HWT-0164, HWT-0165.

### Performance rule

He should speak as someone carrying grave verified news, not as an omniscient author figure. The court should visibly distinguish Kuravar witness from Sāttanār’s structured narration.

### Relationship function

- Kuravar → Sāttanār and court: local witness establishes that Kaṇṇaki reached Cēra country.
- Sāttanār → Senguttuvan: supplies the causal account that makes state response possible.

### Caution

Do not merge Sāttanār with Māṭalan or turn him into a modern historian. His authority comes from narrative knowledge within the epic’s world.

## Iḷaṅkō Vēṇmāḷ — royal consort and policy initiator

### Textual function

- **[TEXT]** Senguttuvan asks the queen to compare the Pāṇḍya queen’s death with Kaṇṇaki’s arrival in Cēra country.
- **[TEXT]** Iḷaṅkō Vēṇmāḷ recommends honouring Kaṇṇaki as Pattini.
- **[TEXT]** Her proposal converts sympathy into a specific royal memorial policy.

**Evidence:** HWT-0166, HWT-0167.

### Performance rule

Her response should arise from listening, comparison and judgement. She is not merely confirming the king’s idea; she gives the court its chosen ethical direction.

### Relationship function

- Sāttanār → Iḷaṅkō Vēṇmāḷ: transmitted catastrophe becomes a question of appropriate honour.
- Iḷaṅkō Vēṇmāḷ → Senguttuvan: proposes the Pattini institution.

### Caution

Do not reduce her to ceremonial approval, emotional softness or decorative queenship. Her speech is a policy intervention.

## Māṭalan — mobile witness, connective memory and ethical counsel

### Textual function

- **[TEXT]** Māṭalan appears in the northern campaign and reconnects Senguttuvan’s victory to Mādhavi, Kōvalaṉ, Kaṇṇaki, Mādhari, Kauṇṭi and both merchant families.
- **[TEXT]** He reports consequences that occurred across Pukār and Madurai after the principal characters separated.
- **[TEXT]** He later counsels Senguttuvan about impermanence, anger, victory and righteous action.

**Evidence:** HWT-0186, HWT-0187, with the broader Canto 27–28 source sequence in `vanji_kaandam.md`.

### Performance rule

Māṭalan should carry the physical and emotional cost of long-distance knowledge. His speech changes scale: private deaths and renunciations interrupt the king’s martial triumph.

### Relationship function

- Māṭalan → Senguttuvan: challenges victory with consequences and ethical limit.
- Māṭalan → dispersed families: joins separate griefs into one remembered causal network.
- Māṭalan → audience: prevents the Vañci campaign from becoming detached spectacle.

### Caution

Do not use him as an unlimited exposition machine. Each report should alter a decision, emotional state or political meaning.

## Senguttuvan — ruler who monumentalises memory

### Textual function

- **[TEXT]** He receives hill testimony and Sāttanār’s account, reflects on royal responsibility and accepts the Pattini proposal.
- **[TEXT]** He converts memorial intent into proclamation, mobilisation, alliance, warfare, stone acquisition and Ganges consecration.
- **[TEXT]** On return he orders civic remission, commissions specialists, installs the image and establishes recurring worship.

**Evidence:** HWT-0166–HWT-0194.

### Performance arc

**listening ruler → morally disturbed sovereign → vow-making king → military organiser → victorious but challengeable ruler → shrine patron and memory administrator.**

### Performance rule

His scale should expand through institutions rather than personal heroics alone: councils, accountants, strategists, heralds, allied rulers, boats, soldiers, ritual experts and craftspeople make the project possible.

### Relationship function

- Kaṇṇaki’s memory → Senguttuvan: provides a moral event he did not create.
- Iḷaṅkō Vēṇmāḷ → Senguttuvan: shapes the form of royal response.
- Māṭalan → Senguttuvan: introduces ethical and human limits to martial glory.
- Senguttuvan → Pattini institution: gives memory durable state-supported form.

### Caution

Do not allow Senguttuvan to replace Kaṇṇaki as protagonist of the epic’s moral truth. The state preserves, enlarges and frames a truth already established through her action and hill-community recognition.

## Memory-flow rules

1. Eyewitness testimony, narrative synthesis and royal policy must remain distinguishable.
2. Hill recognition must precede court monumentalisation.
3. The northern campaign must remain causally linked to the Pattini project.
4. Māṭalan’s reports must interrupt triumph with human consequences.
5. Shrine construction must show collaboration among state, ritual and craft specialists.
6. The final institution must not erase Mādhavi, Kauṇṭi, Mādhari, Dēvanti or the bereaved families whose losses enter its memory.
