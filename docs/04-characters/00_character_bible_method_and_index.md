# Character Bible — Method and Index

## Purpose

This section converts the Tamil-source concordance into performance-ready character dossiers for screenplay, casting, rehearsal, costume, movement and visual development.

The dossiers do not replace the epic with modern psychological biography. They distinguish what the Tamil text states from what cinema may responsibly infer.

## Evidence labels

- **[TEXT]** — directly supported by the original Tamil epic and linked HWT records.
- **[CROSS]** — supported by more than one canto, kāṇṭam or witness within the epic.
- **[INTERPRETATION]** — a controlled dramatic inference proposed for adaptation.
- **[CAUTION]** — a limit preventing stereotype, false certainty or silent modernisation.

## Required character test

Every character choice must answer four questions:

1. **Source:** Which Tamil passage or HWT record supports it?
2. **Arc:** How does it change across Pukār, the road, Madurai and Vañci?
3. **Performance:** What can an actor do physically or vocally without explanatory dialogue?
4. **Limit:** What must the adaptation not claim as textual fact?

## Shared performance principles

- Kaṇṇaki must not begin as a fully formed avenging deity.
- Kōvalaṉ must not be reduced to a simple betrayer or foolish husband.
- Mādhavi must not be staged as a seductress who exists only to derail marriage.
- Kauṇṭi must not become a generic mystical guide or exposition device.
- Social position must be visible through labour, training, household practice, speech access and institutional relationships—not only costume.
- Song, silence, gesture, fatigue, objects and spatial access are character actions.
- The anklet is not merely a symbol: it is property, surviving capital, disputed evidence and the object through which Kaṇṇaki becomes a public legal speaker.
