# Epic Adaptation Structure

## Core approach

Silappathikaram should not be forced into a simple hero journey. Its dramatic engine is the movement of truth:

**abundance → separation → journey → failed justice → moral transformation → collective memory**

## Five movement structure

### Movement I — The Living World (Pukār)

Function:

Establish the world that can be lost.

Key dramatic questions:

- What does Pukār protect?
- What do Kaṇṇaki and Kōvalaṉ possess before loss?
- How does Mādhavi's artistic world operate?

Primary conflict:

Private desire enters a functioning social world.

---

### Movement II — The Road of Consequence

Function:

Turn emotional choices into physical conditions.

Key dramatic questions:

- What does travel reveal?
- What does Kauṇṭi see before others?
- How does landscape test identity?

Primary conflict:

Human intention meets terrain, time and vulnerability.

---

### Movement III — The City of Justice

Function:

Create the central institutional tragedy.

Key dramatic questions:

- How does a city investigate truth?
- What happens when authority acts without examination?
- How does evidence transform the powerless?

Primary conflict:

Truth exists before power recognises it.

---

### Movement IV — Fire and Transformation

Function:

Move Kaṇṇaki from individual grief to moral force.

Key dramatic questions:

- What does destruction mean?
- Who is protected?
- Who remembers?

Primary conflict:

Justice becomes larger than the individual.

---

### Movement V — Memory and Institution

Function:

Show how societies preserve and transmit moral events.

Key dramatic questions:

- Who carries testimony?
- Why does a king build a monument?
- How does a person become cultural memory?

Primary conflict:

Memory must remain connected to the original injustice.
