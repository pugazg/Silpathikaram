## Draft controls

1. Invented dialogue is explicitly identified as adaptation dialogue, not quotation from the Tamil poem.
2. Pukār is introduced as a working river–sea and merchant city rather than a decorative palace world.
3. The marriage establishes kinship, property and public responsibility without inventing a modern courtship.
4. Kaṇṇaki’s household competence is shown before separation and tragedy.
5. The anklet pair is introduced as protected household property; its internal contents remain hidden.
6. The final sound transition leads from the household chest to Mādhavi’s disciplined training in `SCN-PUK-005`.

## Revision gate

Before `draft_v2`, dialogue rhythm, historical material details, marriage reconstruction, harbour work and household architecture require source and production review.
