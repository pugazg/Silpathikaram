## Locked dramatic controls

1. Madurai begins as an organised and desirable capital; competence makes the justice failure more devastating.
2. Kaṇṇaki enters with an injured body and the unopened anklet pair under her control.
3. Kōvalaṉ understands commerce but lacks a local protection network.
4. Mādhari’s household and Kauṇṭi’s protection form a chain of women’s care.
5. Kuravai functions as communal repair and breaks only when the death report arrives.
6. One anklet goes to market; Kaṇṇaki retains the other and remains owner of both.
7. The goldsmith’s expert classification enters the state before ownership, transaction purpose, pair or contents are tested.
8. Royal procedure converts an untested claim into irreversible violence.
9. Kōvalaṉ’s death is wrongful state action, not karmic punishment for earlier relational failures.
10. Grief becomes a civic and legal claim without ceasing to be grief.
11. Citizens remain differentiated witnesses, not an instant organised protest.
12. Kōvalaṉ’s body is material evidence of failed procedure.
13. The queen’s dream registers failing royal order but does not reveal the case or substitute for proof.
14. The queen is not Kaṇṇaki’s rival; she participates in and suffers the collapse of royal authority.
15. The gate receives Kaṇṇaki as an evidence-bearing accuser, not merely a grieving petitioner.
16. Kaṇṇaki establishes standing through identity, family, ownership, marriage and commercial purpose.
17. The decisive distinction is material: the queen’s anklet contains pearls; Kaṇṇaki’s contains gems.
18. Kaṇṇaki controls the test and breaks the anklet herself.
19. The gem impact is physical evidence, not magical action.
20. Royal recognition arrives only after irreversible harm.
21. The fallen sceptre indicts the governing order, not only one man’s conscience.
22. The king’s self-indictment is not restitution and does not restore Kōvalaṉ.
23. The primary interval may occur only after the gem proof and royal collapse at minute `117.0`.
24. `SCN-MDU-020` hands directly into Kaṇṇaki’s city-level vow and remembered-women sequence.
