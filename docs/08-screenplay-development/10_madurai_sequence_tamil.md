## Locked dramatic controls

1. Madurai begins as an organised, maintained and desirable capital whose visible competence intensifies its later justice failure.
2. The gate, moat, wall, flags, guards, transport and registration systems function before the state’s procedural collapse.
3. Kaṇṇaki enters with an injured body and with the unopened anklet pair under her control.
4. Kōvalaṉ can read markets and craft systems, but he has no local merchant network, witness or protected introduction.
5. River, market, performance, ritual, animal, artisan and palace-service systems form one connected urban economy.
6. Madurai’s artistic sound may recall Mādhavi without implying that she is physically present.
7. Kauṇṭi prioritises Kaṇṇaki’s body and shelter over Kōvalaṉ’s urgency to reach the market.
8. Mādhari’s household is a functioning pastoral institution, not a poverty symbol or generic maternal sanctuary.
9. Kauṇṭi’s handover is a practical transfer from ascetic route protection to household care.
10. Kaṇṇaki’s travel injury is cleaned and rested but not erased.
11. Kōvalaṉ’s recognition of lost family, wealth and protection does not absolve his prior choices.
12. Kaṇṇaki remains materially and verbally authoritative about ownership and the anklet sale.
13. Cattle and dairy irregularities are first tested through ordinary work knowledge before becoming shared ritual concern.
14. The Kuravai is communal repair through coordinated performance, not decorative interruption or certain prophecy.
15. The Kuravai continues as an intercut action through the market, accusation and execution; it must break only when the death news reaches `SCN-MDU-010`.
16. Kaṇṇaki gives one anklet to Kōvalaṉ for sale while retaining the other. Both remain unopened and their contents unrevealed.
17. Kōvalaṉ is the sale agent; Kaṇṇaki remains the owner.
18. Kōvalaṉ’s market vulnerability comes from missing local protection, not from ignorance of commerce or craft.
19. The goldsmith converts specialised expertise and palace-linked credibility into false classification.
20. Other artisans are not implicated collectively; the catastrophe begins with one specialist’s deception entering a system that fails to test it.
21. “Thief” reaches the state before owner, transaction purpose, witness, pair, contents or place and time of theft are examined.
22. The royal order moves faster than inquiry and predetermines the officers’ action.
23. Kōvalaṉ attempts to state his name, Kaṇṇaki’s ownership, the paired anklet and the sale purpose, but the procedure does not receive his speech.
24. Kōvalaṉ’s death is wrongful state violence, not deserved karmic punishment for his earlier relational failures.
25. The sale anklet enters state custody intact; its gem contents remain concealed until Kaṇṇaki’s court test.
26. Mādhavi’s flower-letter remains a separately tracked object on or near Kōvalaṉ’s body.
27. Kōvalaṉ’s body must remain traceable into `SCN-MDU-013`, where citizens and Kaṇṇaki confront the material result of royal procedure.
28. `SCN-MDU-009` ends with city rumour moving toward the pastoral settlement and hands directly into the cry that breaks the Kuravai.
