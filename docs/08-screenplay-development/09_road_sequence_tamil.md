## Locked dramatic controls

1. The Road begins as a direct continuation of Kaṇṇaki’s first uncertain step outside Pukār.
2. Terrain removes social confidence through feet, load, water and pace rather than through generic danger spectacle.
3. Kāviri country is a working irrigation and agrarian system, not an effortless pastoral background.
4. Distance is measured through halts, water, shade and bodily recovery.
5. Kaṇṇaki’s initial foot pressure must develop gradually into later redness, swelling and injury.
6. Kauṇṭi’s care is practical, observant and consent-aware; she does not become an omniscient oracle.
7. The river-island teaching may deepen ethical context but must never excuse Madurai’s later institutional failure as deserved fate.
8. Precise doctrinal vocabulary, teacher identity and ascetic material culture remain under source and historical review.
9. River-crossing technology remains `RESEARCH_HOLD`; the scene locks bodily exposure and loss of protection rather than a speculative vehicle.
10. Kaṇṇaki retains control of the anklet through the crossing, and its contents remain concealed.
11. The south bank removes shelter and leads directly into the competing-route debate of `SCN-ROAD-006`.
