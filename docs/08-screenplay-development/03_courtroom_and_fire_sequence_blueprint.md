# Courtroom and Fire Sequence Blueprint

## Courtroom sequence

## Dramatic function

The court is not a debate between equals. It is the moment when evidence enters an institution that failed to investigate.

## Sequence logic

1. Kōvalaṉ is removed from the story before truth is heard.
2. Kaṇṇaki enters as a private person carrying public evidence.
3. The anklet becomes the central forensic object.
4. Material difference reveals institutional failure.
5. Royal recognition arrives after irreversible harm.

## Locked decisions

- The anklet must be physically examined.
- The gem/pearl distinction must remain clear.
- Kaṇṇaki's speech must come from evidence and grief.
- The king's collapse must follow recognition of error.

# Fire sequence

## Dramatic function

Fire is the consequence of failed justice transformed into cosmic and civic rupture.

## Sequence logic

1. Kaṇṇaki's grief becomes vow.
2. The city witnesses moral judgement.
3. Fire is selective, not random spectacle.
4. Protected categories remain visible.
5. Madurai's destruction becomes remembered history.

## Locked decisions

- Avoid treating destruction as simple revenge fantasy.
- Preserve the distinction between guilty institutions and protected people.
- Connect fire to justice, memory and consequence.
