# Dialogue and Voice Philosophy

## Principle

Dialogue must preserve the epic's relationship between speech, silence, song and testimony.

Characters should not speak as modern people wearing historical costumes.

## Voice systems

### Kaṇṇaki

Movement:

- restrained household speech;
- practical judgement;
- compressed grief;
- formal accusation;
- moral declaration.

Rule:

Her power emerges from clarity, not volume alone.

---

### Kōvalaṉ

Movement:

- merchant confidence;
- artistic sensitivity;
- emotional uncertainty;
- shame;
- acceptance of responsibility.

Rule:

Do not reduce him to villain or innocent victim.

---

### Mādhavi

Movement:

- technical artistic language;
- coded performance;
- intimate conversation;
- written appeal.

Rule:

Her art is a communication system.

---

### Kauṇṭi

Movement:

- observation;
- ethical questioning;
- practical instruction;
- protective command.

Rule:

She guides without predicting everything.

---

### Royal voices

Pāṇḍya court:

Language of authority, procedure and judgement.

Cēra court:

Language of reflection, responsibility and memorialisation.

## Song adaptation

Songs are not interruptions. They are actions.

A song may:

- reveal knowledge;
- conceal intention;
- challenge another character;
- preserve memory.

## Silence

Silence should carry:

- shame;
- waiting;
- grief;
- institutional failure;
- sacred recognition.
