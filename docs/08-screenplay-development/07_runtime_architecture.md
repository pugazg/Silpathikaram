# Feature Runtime Architecture

## Governing principle

*Silappatikāram* is not compressed into a conventional hero journey. Runtime follows the movement of truth:

**abundance → separation → journey → failed justice → transformation → memory**

## Working cut

- **Total runtime:** 180 minutes
- **Screenplay target:** approximately 180 pages
- **Scene units:** 102
- **Primary interval:** after `SCN-MDU-020`, when material proof has collapsed the Pāṇḍya court
- **Interval position:** approximately minute 117
- **Pacing rule:** source-critical dramatic function controls duration; scenes are not assigned equal length

## Movement allocation

| Movement | Scene range | Minutes | Film position | Dramatic purpose |

| V — Vañci | `SCN-VAN-001`–`SCN-VAN-030` | 42 | 138–180 | Show local recognition, testimony, royal institutionalisation and ethical closure |

## Interval lock

The interval follows the gem striking the king, the king’s self-indictment and the queen’s collapse. It must not occur before the anklet is tested.

This placement protects the causal order:

1. Kōvalaṉ is wrongfully killed.
2. Kaṇṇaki creates a public claim.
3. The court hears identity and material difference.
4. The anklet supplies proof.
5. Royal authority recognises its own failure.
6. Only then does the story widen from court judgement to city judgement.

## Compression priorities

### Priority A — protect in every cut

- Kaṇṇaki and Kōvalaṉ’s merchant-house origin;
- Mādhavi’s artistic institution and the Kāṉal Vari misunderstanding;
- anklet as property, capital and evidence;
- Kauṇṭi’s ethical route choice;
- wrongful execution without adequate investigation;
- gem-versus-pearl proof;
- selective-fire categories;
- local hill recognition before Cēra action;
- Māṭalan’s reconnection of victory to earlier losses;
- ethical conclusion.

### Priority B — compress but do not erase

- festival and stage-infrastructure scale;
- agrarian travel systems;
- sacred-route description;
- pastoral omen and kuravai detail;
- queen’s dream;
- remembered faithful women;
- expedition provisioning and diplomatic administration;
- landscape songs of return.
