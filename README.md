## Translation approach

- Source order and section divisions are preserved.
- Verse is rendered as verse or block quotation; prose remains prose.
- Proper names and technical Tamil literary, musical, religious, and performance terms are transliterated when an ordinary English replacement would erase important meaning.
- The translations aim for close literary English rather than a loose summary.
- Source attribution and Project Madurai notices are retained.

## Source-completeness note for *Silambō Silambu!*

The supplied Project Madurai e-text breaks off during Chapter 17 and resumes at Chapter 20 under an embedded Part 2 header. The remainder of Chapter 17 and the whole of Chapters 18–19 are absent from that source. The translation marks these omissions explicitly and does not invent missing text. The prose of Chapter 26 is present, although its heading is absent in the body; the heading has been restored from the source table of contents.

## Source and redistribution

The Tamil e-texts were prepared and distributed by Project Madurai, whose source headers permit free redistribution when attribution/header information is retained. The critical work’s printed-source notice states that rights belong to its author. This repository is a scholarly English translation project and retains the supplied bibliographic and attribution information.
