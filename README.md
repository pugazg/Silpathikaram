# Silapathikaram — Source-Traceable Cinematic Adaptation Archive

A research and development archive for a serious cinematic adaptation of Iḷaṅkō Aṭikaḷ’s *Silapathikaram* (*Cilappatikāram*).

## Project goal

> **Build a source-traceable adaptation archive supporting research, screenplay development, character performance, storyboards, production design and scholarly review.**

## Controlling primary source

The controlling source for every major narrative, character, visual, musical, philosophical and production decision is the **original Tamil text of all three kāṇṭams**:

1. `pukaar_kaandam.md` — **Pukār Kāṇṭam**, cantos 1–10;
2. `madurai_kaandam.md` — **Madurai Kāṇṭam**, cantos 11–23;
3. `vanji_kaandam.md` — **Vañji Kāṇṭam**, cantos 24–30 and the concluding sections.

English translations, traditional commentaries, modern criticism, historical research and comparative texts may clarify the Tamil text, but they must not silently replace it. Where editions or interpretations differ, the disagreement must be recorded rather than hidden.

Every substantive research or adaptation record should identify the kāṇṭam, canto, Tamil passage or line group, evidence type, verification status and any interpretive change made for cinema.

## Core adaptation principles

The [full source-governed principles](docs/01-vision/02_core_adaptation_principles.md) are binding on all later work. Their core requirements are:

1. **Preserve the epic’s declared threefold moral design.** The adaptation must hold together:
   - **“அரசியல் பிழைத்தோர்க்கு அறம்கூற்று ஆவதூஉம்”** — failed government is answerable to moral law;
   - **“உரைசால் பத்தினிக்கு உயர்ந்தோர் ஏத்தலும்”** — Kaṇṇaki’s truth and steadfastness receive public recognition;
   - **“ஊழ்வினை உருத்துவந்து ஊட்டும் என்பதூஉம்”** — past action and causation shape events.

2. **Treat the three kāṇṭams as one indivisible causal work.** Pukār is not merely a romantic prelude, Madurai is not the whole story, and Vañji is not an optional ceremonial epilogue.

3. **Preserve the epic’s mixed dramatic form.** *Silapathikaram* is **“உரையிடை இட்ட பாட்டுடைச் செய்யுள்”**: prose, verse, song, dance, lament, ritual, landscape and legal argument all perform narrative work.

4. **Keep the anklet as the material and evidentiary spine.** It is intimate property, remaining wealth, an object of false identification, legal proof, sound, material difference and the title-bearing symbol.

5. **Build Pukār as a functioning world.** Its maritime economy, merchant households, labour, performance institutions, festivals, streets and sea must shape the characters’ choices.

6. **Treat performance as action, not decoration.** Mādhavi’s training, debut, artistic labour and the *Kāṉal Vari* must affect relationships, misunderstanding and causation.

7. **Preserve character complexity.** Kaṇṇaki, Kōvalaṉ and Mādhavi must not be reduced respectively to a passive ideal, a simple betrayer or a seductress.

8. **Let Kaṇṇaki’s silence accumulate dramatic force.** Her public speech in Madurai must emerge from lived experience, knowledge of the anklet, grief and the failure of authority.

9. **Make travel and landscape causal.** Heat, thirst, fatigue, ecology, routes, settlements and encounters must visibly transform the travellers.

10. **Centre the Madurai crisis on failure to investigate.** The force of Kaṇṇaki’s **“தேரா மன்னா”** and the anklet’s public proof must remain central.

11. **Treat the burning of Madurai as moral and human catastrophe.** It must not become uncomplicated revenge spectacle, and the Tamil text’s stated exemptions and limits must be respected.

12. **Preserve social and religious plurality.** Merchants, performers, ascetics, hunters, cowherds, hill communities, craftspeople, officials and multiple sacred traditions must not be flattened into one generic culture.

13. **Show local recognition before royal monumentalisation.** The hill communities first recognise and ritualise Kaṇṇaki’s sacred status; the Cēra state later converts testimony into a memorial project.

14. **Treat Vañji as the politics of memory.** Stone, journey, warfare, water consecration, installation and ritual must reveal how royal power preserves, enlarges and also frames Kaṇṇaki’s memory.

15. **Preserve tension between அறம், அரசியல் பிழை, ஊழ்வினை and human responsibility.** Fate must not excuse government, supernatural causation must not erase human choice, and modern rationalisation must not erase the epic’s sacred dimension.

16. **Allow cinema to transform form, but never falsify source.** Condensation, visual transitions and controlled interpretation are permitted; reversing Tamil-source outcomes, inventing evidence, erasing a kāṇṭam or presenting disputed reconstruction as textual fact is not.

### Five binding tests

Every later research, screenplay, character, storyboard or production-design decision must pass:

1. **Tamil-source test** — Can it be traced to an original Tamil passage or explicitly labelled as interpretation?
2. **Three-kāṇṭam test** — Does it preserve the causal unity of Pukār, Madurai and Vañji?
3. **Moral-design test** — Does it retain political accountability, Kaṇṇaki’s public truth and causation without allowing one to erase the others?
4. **Dramatic-form test** — Does it respect the integration of speech, song, performance, landscape, ritual and legal argument?
5. **Cinematic-honesty test** — Does it create effective cinema without falsely claiming that invention is present in the Tamil text?

## Source-completeness note for *Silambō Silambu!*

The supplied Project Madurai e-text breaks off during Chapter 17 and resumes at Chapter 20 under an embedded Part 2 header. The remainder of Chapter 17 and the whole of Chapters 18–19 are absent from that source. The translation marks these omissions explicitly and does not invent missing text. The prose of Chapter 26 is present, although its heading is absent in the body; the heading has been restored from the source table of contents.

## Source and redistribution

The Tamil e-texts were prepared and distributed by Project Madurai, whose source headers permit free redistribution when attribution and header information are retained. The critical work’s printed-source notice states that rights belong to its author. This repository retains the supplied bibliographic and attribution information and distinguishes primary text, secondary scholarship and cinematic interpretation.
